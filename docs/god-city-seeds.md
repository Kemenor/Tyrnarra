**Process:** full workflow per city, one at a time. **Build order started with Haizava** (Wind), because the god-city pass grew out of the Vindul depth-audit. City *names* are provisional; propose a better-fitting name per god at build time if one exists. The byname is the descriptor.

**Status legend:** *seed locked* = the seed and uniqueness are final, ready for a full-workflow build; *building* = in progress; *done* = lore (and HTML) committed. Every unbuilt city below is seed-locked.

## Uniqueness architecture

The 2-sentence test governs: a reader should know which city a description names within two sentences.

- **The elemental quartet** are four answers to *change*: Eldara **forges** (creation through the friction of method-vs-recklessness), Uravel **flows** (adapt or drown), Haizava **is rebuilt by the wind** (read it, do not map it), Lurrath **endures** (refuses to change at all).
- **The make/build/know cluster** (the easiest to blur): Nahaskel **erupts** (mortal madness, undisciplined, ephemeral, nothing lasts); Eldara **builds** (disciplined-by-its-other-half, ships lasting machines); Thekkavar **learns** (knows, teaches, keeps). Three verbs, three domains.
- **Haizava vs Nahaskel** (the sharpest call): Haizava changes because *the wind does it to the city* (elemental, beautiful, beyond control). Nahaskel changes because *its people will not stop doing it to themselves* (mortal, mad, generative). Wind-machine vs mad-workshop.
- **The light-dark pair:** Ljosarn **exposes** (eternal light, nothing hides); Myrria **hides and forgives** (shadow, second chances).
- **Frae City vs Nahaskel** ("no rules" could rhyme): Frae City is *free* (the people are sane and deliberately ungoverned, principled anti-authority); Nahaskel is *mad* (reality itself will not hold still). Liberty vs chaos.
- **Thekkavar vs Azkataria vs Atarialda** (knowledge): Azkataria **argues** it (open-floor agora); Thekkavar **teaches and keeps** it (academy and stacks); Atarialda **remembers** it (oral, in the kitchen, the anti-library). Thekkavar is the formal-institutional pole.

## Per-city build notes

- **Eldara** — the dwarf-and-kobold dual-engine forge (New Age Dwarves draw schematics and build steady; Kobolds blow up five prototypes rather than draw five drawings; cooperation, when they can stop arguing, makes wonders, and the argument between the two tempers is the city's daily weather; nothing here is made by one hand. Komo's Destruction lives in the kobold-side, the prototypes that blow up on the way to the one that works). **Build flag:** must read distinct from the **Order of Steam** (also Sumendar, also dwarf-and-kobold). Lean: Eldara is Komo's *sacred forge* and bleeding edge where making is worship; the Order of Steam is the mortal industrial kingdom that mass-produces.
- **Thekkavar** — the university-city with more institutes of learning than anywhere on Talan, where every facet of life is learnable (theater, music, cooking, arcana, not only the math and the arcane), because Enki's Occult spark lives in the recipe, the song, and the lullaby as much as the equation, and the city holds more of those spark-bearing stories than anyone; the libraries are the grandest and deepest on the continent, the clergy's own bottomless, and Enki himself may be lecturing on ancient history (keeping his own counsel on the parts he withholds). Everyday learning is open and expansive; the sealed shelves and withheld lore are the **Mysteries** (a GM-tier seam).
- **Denbora** — nomad city on titanic sandwhales roaming endless desert; halts a night, a week, or as long as it takes to dig out ruins the wind unburies (old Denbora itself, scattered when **Tani died**, recovered piece by piece). Greatest whale bears the sanctum, noble quarter, and palace; lesser whales the other districts; in transit the herd draws close and the people throw contraption-bridges across, and a drifted whale can leave you two days from home (the citizens find this funny). "Lives in three tenses": recovers the past, rides the present, builds the future. **Build flag:** pre-read `timeline.md` (Tani's death, the Storveldi Denbora) and `lioaru.md` (Blackened Lands, Galdua Jendea / the sand-whale roaming-range) end-to-end first.
- **Haizava** — the city the wind physically rebuilds (sails, vanes, counterweights, rope-swung bridges; you hire a wind-reader, not a map); the one place the **Wyndwalken** cartographers' maps cannot fix, which makes Vindul hold both the masters of mapping and the city that defeats them. Already read for build: `vindul.md`, Fisaya's god-sheet, glossary Vindul block.
- **Lograth** — needs the deeper dive past its Thousand-Kingdom framing (it sits within the Thousand Kingdom, Zuzental, the reference-implementation of Forseti's law).
- **Frae City** — at Askamira, Cronus's island-domain ("all ancestries equally"); Cronus was mortal and his worshippers are made by choice (GM secret). Needs a byname at build.
