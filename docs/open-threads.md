# Open Threads: Tyrnarra

Status-based punch list for canon work. Each gate represents how far along a thread is.

**How to use this file:**
- *Gate* tells you what kind of work is needed next.
- *Topic* (in the entry header) shows which canon domain it belongs to.
- *Decided* prevents future sessions from re-litigating settled bits.
- *Open* is the specific work to do.
- *Where* points to the file(s) where the thread is currently anchored.

When you close a thread, move the entry to **§ Resolved** and strike it through with `~~...~~` (so the resolution context survives). If closing it publishes new HTML, also update [`site-inventory.md`](site-inventory.md).

The day-to-day texture of that anchoring (do they feel it? do priests detect it? do clerical grants work on them?) is open.

- **Relationship to Betibizi's integration procedure.** The Storveldi route bypasses the Compact by ritual; the Reflection route bypasses it by birth. Both produce *Wellspring-direct* beings. A Reflection who learns of the integration procedure is the Golden Emperor's path.

### [Cosmology] Gods' Law follow-ups after the Compact commit
**Decided.** The full Compact-and-Articles structure is canon ([cosmology.md](../lore/cosmology.md), *The Gods' Law: The Compact of the Bound Thirteen*). The Iro / Drambur Crimson-Rain incident is the only canon example currently (Iro cannot speak against his own zealots because of the Drambur wound; voluntary, Law-enforced, or both?). ### [Campaign Hook] The Golden Emperor Returns
**Decided.** The Golden Emperor was a Reflection (Stillpool-born, Wellspring-direct) who learned to consciously pull on his Wellspring connection and ascended to Minor God status on the Material Plane via the loophole that the Gods' Law's deity-mediation framework leaves open for Wellspring-direct beings. He deliberately ordered the 1321 MR breach of the Corrupted God's seal as an attempted ascent fuel-source, miscalculated catastrophically, but survived the breach and personally coordinated the Imperial defence, even concentrating his power directly against the Corrupted God. The concentration starved the periphery; the Empire cracked and formally collapsed in 1325 MR. He kept fighting in shrinking territory for years after. A **rebellion party** eventually surprised him after a defensive engagement against another spawn-wave and killed him. At the moment of death he **captured his own soul into a pocket dimension within Layer 2**, the Postlife bypass, same Wellspring-direct mechanism that had bypassed the Compact at his ascent. He has been in that pocket for roughly twelve hundred years, **continuously seeking a path to resurrect.** Full canon in [timeline.md](../lore/timeline.md), *Golden Era → ⚿ GM Secret: The Golden Empire: The Real History* and *Dark Era → ⚿ GM Secret: The Dark Era: The Mining Wasn't Accidental*.
**Open.** Develop the campaign frame.
- *Trigger.* What sets his return in motion now? An archaeologist breaching an Imperial-era ruin? A Reflection PC stumbling onto the same loophole he found? A Stillpool encounter that someone in his pocket can sense? An adventurer party that uncovers his personal records in an old mining-province archive?
- *Return shape.* Does he come back as a **benevolent ascendant** trying to finish what he started against the Corrupted God? As a **vengeful figure** with twelve centuries of pocket-dimension refinement turned inward? Both, with no easy player-side reading? A campaign that forces players to choose which version they help, and discovers too late which they got.
- *What the Adventurers Guild can see.* The Guild's sealed-Stillpool atlas plus its founding-tier records of the Dark Era are the closest mortal institutions to evidence. The Guild may know something the bound thirteen literally cannot.
- *What's hidden from the thirteen.* The Compact's deity-mediation framework blinds the thirteen to Wellspring-direct activity; an Emperor's return-event happens in the gap their instruments don't reach. This is the campaign's structural asymmetry: players have access the gods don't.
- *Artefact-trail.* What evidence of his original ascent surfaces as clues? Imperial-era documents that show the mining programme's true bearings; a Stillpool the Emperor's mother passed; the rebellion-party's recorded testimony (if any); the shrine-or-monument the Empire built that's actually a divine-engineering site in disguise; etcetera.
- *Tie-ins.* Stillpools (his origin), Imperial mining-site ruins (his trigger), Reflections in the modern day (his kin and his recruitment pool).
**Where.** Future write-up location TBD: possibly a new `lore/campaigns.md` file, or new sub-sections in the destination files. Cross-refs to [lore/timeline.md](../lore/timeline.md), *Golden Era* and *Dark Era* GM-tier sub-sections.
