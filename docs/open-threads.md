# Open Threads — Tyrnarra

Status-based punch list for canon work. Each gate represents how far along a thread is.

**How to use this file:**
- *Gate* tells you what kind of work is needed next.
- *Topic* (in the entry header) shows which canon domain it belongs to.
- *Decided* prevents future sessions from re-litigating settled bits.
- *Open* is the specific work to do.
- *Where* points to the file(s) where the thread is currently anchored.

When you close a thread, move the entry to **§ Resolved** and strike it through with `~~...~~` (so the resolution context survives). If closing it publishes new HTML, also update [`site-inventory.md`](site-inventory.md).

## § Campaign hooks — story frames waiting on a write-up

Items where the canon is locked but the *story-shaping* — the campaign frame, the threats, the player-facing structure — is the work remaining. Distinct from §Needs writing (where the canon question itself is open).

### [Campaign Hook] The Golden Emperor Returns
**Decided.** The Golden Emperor was a Reflection (Stillpool-born, Wellspring-direct) who learned to consciously pull on his Wellspring connection and ascended to Minor God status on the Material Plane via the loophole that the Gods' Law's deity-mediation framework leaves open for Wellspring-direct beings. He deliberately ordered the 1321 MR breach of the Corrupted God's seal as an attempted ascent fuel-source, miscalculated catastrophically, but survived the breach and personally coordinated the Imperial defence — even concentrating his power directly against the Corrupted God. The concentration starved the periphery; the Empire cracked and formally collapsed in 1325 MR. He kept fighting in shrinking territory for years after. A **rebellion party** eventually surprised him after a defensive engagement against another spawn-wave and killed him. At the moment of death he **captured his own soul into a pocket dimension within Layer 2** — the Postlife bypass, same Wellspring-direct mechanism that had bypassed the Compact at his ascent. He has been in that pocket for roughly twelve hundred years, **continuously seeking a path to resurrect.** Full canon in [secret-history.md](../lore/secret-history.md) — *The Golden Empire — The Real History* and *The Dark Era — The Mining Wasn't Accidental*.
**Open.** Develop the campaign frame.
- *Trigger.* What sets his return in motion now? An archaeologist breaching an Imperial-era ruin? A Reflection PC stumbling onto the same loophole he found? A Stillpool encounter that someone in his pocket can sense? An adventurer party that uncovers his personal records in an old mining-province archive?
- *Return shape.* Does he come back as a **benevolent ascendant** trying to finish what he started against the Corrupted God? As a **vengeful figure** with twelve centuries of pocket-dimension refinement turned inward? Both, with no easy player-side reading? A campaign that forces players to choose which version they help — and discovers too late which they got.
- *What the Adventurers Guild can see.* The Guild's sealed-Stillpool atlas plus its founding-tier records of the Dark Era are the closest mortal institutions to evidence. The Guild may know something the bound thirteen literally cannot.
- *What's hidden from the thirteen.* The Compact's deity-mediation framework blinds the thirteen to Wellspring-direct activity; an Emperor's return-event happens in the gap their instruments don't reach. This is the campaign's structural asymmetry — players have access the gods don't.
- *Artefact-trail.* What evidence of his original ascent surfaces as clues? Imperial-era documents that show the mining programme's true bearings; a Stillpool the Emperor's mother passed; the rebellion-party's recorded testimony (if any); the shrine-or-monument the Empire built that's actually a divine-engineering site in disguise; etcetera.
- *Tie-ins.* Stillpools (his origin), Imperial mining-site ruins (his trigger), Reflections in the modern day (his kin and his recruitment pool).
**Where.** Future write-up location TBD — possibly a new `lore/campaigns.md` file, or a dedicated section in `secret-history.md`. Cross-refs to [secret-history.md](../lore/secret-history.md) Golden + Dark sections.

## § Don't reintroduce — canon corrections that must not be undone

Sortalde freight arrives via **cloudship** and lands on the **Bridgelands** (Emerald Isles' NE outer-rim islands). No Magitrain line connects to Sortalde; no train freight runs the Sortalde route; **Train Pirates do not have a "Sortalde-route specialist" category.** Mid-session correction 2026-05-17; the page text was cleaned up the same day. Do not reintroduce any rail-Sortalde wording.

### Sortalde embassies: on Emerald Isles' Bridgelands, not Merkavar or Crossroads
Sortalde embassies are **NOT in Merkavar or Crossroads** (those are inland). They are on the **Emerald Isles' Bridgelands** — the NE outer-rim islands of the Zuzental island kingdom, partially in Hafra / partially in Cloud Sea, connected by spanning Magitech bridges. Mid-session correction 2026-05-17; pages updated the same day.
**Where this lives correctly.** [emerald-isles.html](../talan/domains/zuzental/emerald-isles.html) — *The Bridgelands*; [off-continent/sortalde.html](../off-continent/sortalde.html) — embassy section; cross-links on [lautara.html](../talan/domains/lautara/lautara.html) and [crossroads.html](../talan/domains/lautara/crossroads.html).

### No "Hooks" / "Adventure Seeds" sections on published HTML
Published HTML is **lore documentation, not campaign-starter material**. Dedicated *Hooks* or *Adventure Seeds* sections with campaign prompts ("a campaign that crosses the two…", "a Yaksha exile whose bond was broken could open…") belong in private GM notes, not on the site. World-flavour expandables (folkloric ◈ Popular Belief, in-world tavern rumours, "what people say" speculation) are different and welcome — they characterise *the world*, not *what to do in it*. This rule also lives in [CLAUDE.md](../CLAUDE.md) and in user memory.
