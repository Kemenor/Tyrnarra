# Open Threads — Tyrnarra

Everything currently flagged as TBD, unresolved, or awaiting development. Maintained as a punch-list for sessions picking up canon work.

**How to use this file:**
- *Status* tells you how locked-in surrounding canon is — high means several other entries depend on this thread, low means it's isolated.
- *What's decided* prevents future sessions from re-litigating settled bits.
- *What's open* is the specific work to do.
- *Where it lives* points to the file(s) where the thread is currently anchored.

When you close a thread, strike it through with `~~...~~` rather than deleting (so the resolution context survives), and update [docs/worldanvil-migration.md](worldanvil-migration.md)'s resume point.

## Cosmology & Gods

### The Sun god
**Status:** medium — referenced but undefined.
**What's decided:** Mortals have prayed to Solyra (the sun) for as long as there have been mortals. By the same mechanism that birthed **Bikiargi** from prayer to the two moons, a sun-deity should plausibly exist.
**What's open:** Whether there is a single Sun god, whether they're absorbed into Iro's Light portfolio, whether they're another Layer-3 resident parallel to Bikiargi, and (if standalone) their name.
**Where it lives:** [lore/world-notes.md](../lore/world-notes.md) — *Named Non-Bound Gods → Open thread — The Sun*.

### Sortalde Layer-3 pantheon
**Status:** high — anchors Sortalde's clerical substrate.
**What's decided:** Sortalde has *no walking gods.* All clerical work channels through Layer-3-resident gods only. The pantheon abstractly comprises **dynasty-spirits, ancestor-judges, and place-gods**. Religion on Sortalde is invocation across the veil, never personal audition.
**What's open:** Names and individual portfolios for the pantheon. A small naming pass (3–5 cornerstone gods) would close the religious texture of the Eastern continent.
**Where it lives:** [lore/world-notes.md](../lore/world-notes.md) — *Other Pantheons*; [lore/geography.md](../lore/geography.md) — *Other Continents → Sortalde*.

### Sin-Devil names + 4 missing grantable domains
**Status:** medium — the seven-devil structure is locked, the named bindings are partial.
**What's decided:** Seven canonical Sin Devils (Envy, Gluttony, Greed, Lust, Pride, Sloth, Wrath). Three are bound to grantable domains via the *Domains Outside the Thirteen* mapping: **Gluttony→Indulgence, Pride→Tyranny, Wrath→Destruction.**
**What's open:** Individual devil-lord names (all seven), planar seats, politics, relationships with mortals. Grantable domains for **Envy, Greed, Lust, Sloth** (the four unmapped devils — what 4 PF2e domains they grant on Talan).
**Where it lives:** [lore/bestiary.md](../lore/bestiary.md) — *Sin Devils — the Seven*; [lore/world-notes.md](../lore/world-notes.md) — *Domains Outside the Thirteen*.

### Individual Virtue-Demon names beyond Muiral
**Status:** low — rolling worldbuilding hook.
**What's decided:** The Aristotelian virtue/excess/deficiency pattern is locked. **Muiral the Misshapen** (Modesty-line, Shamelessness/Shyness) grants the **Abomination** domain. **Pain → Patience-line excess**, **Toil → Industriousness-line excess**, **Sorrow → Magnanimity-line deficiency**, **Delirium → Temperance-line deficiency** (each as an unnamed demon).
**What's open:** Individual names for the other virtue-line demons as they become relevant in play. No batch-naming pass needed — let them name themselves when stories call for them.
**Where it lives:** [lore/bestiary.md](../lore/bestiary.md) — *Virtue Demons — the Aristotelian Pattern*.

### Vampire origin on Talan
**Status:** medium — a lineage with no source story.
**What's decided:** Dhampir reframed as undead-touched (not god-touched), which makes vampires-as-an-independent-lineage canon. Vampires exist on Talan and produce Dhampir descendants.
**What's open:** Where vampires came from. Candidate hooks:
- **Failed Betibizi-integration attempts** (strongest candidate — Betibizi holds the integration procedure; failed integrations could produce vampire-state rather than full ascension)
- Blackened Lands émigrés (Storveldi-Denbora-descended)
- Banned-god creations (a god expelled by Gods' Law who left vampires behind)
- Feyworld émigrés
**Where it lives:** [lore/bestiary.md](../lore/bestiary.md) — Dhampir heritage; [docs/worldanvil-migration.md](worldanvil-migration.md) resume point.

### The order Grand Gods joined Cronus during the Week of Crimson Rain
**Status:** low — narrative texture.
**What's decided:** Cronus united all eleven remaining Grand Gods. **Araphel** was the first to stand behind him.
**What's open:** The sequence of the other ten and the political moments that decided each.
**Where it lives:** [lore/world-notes.md](../lore/world-notes.md) — *The Week of Crimson Rain — The True Account*.

### The Devourer (the Void) — ending mechanism
**Status:** low — cosmological dangling thread.
**What's decided:** The Devourer cannot be permanently killed by conventional means — it contains both Wellspring and Void.
**What's open:** What it would actually take to end it. The thought is canonically not-written-down in any library.
**Where it lives:** [lore/world-notes.md](../lore/world-notes.md) — *The Devourer (The Void)*.

### The Elden — fuller origin, nature, current state
**Status:** low — GM-truth backdrop.
**What's decided:** They were a civilisation in the Elden Era (6000 GR – 2945 GR). They became the Corrupted God. Mortals do not know this.
**What's open:** Detail of who they were before becoming the Corrupted God, what civilisation they built, why they fused with the Devourer.
**Where it lives:** [lore/world-notes.md](../lore/world-notes.md) — *The Elden* and *The Corrupted God — True Identity*.

## Geography & Continents

### The Red Empire's home continent
**Status:** high — the Red Empire is fully developed as a faction, but their home base is unmapped.
**What's decided:** It is across Hafra. Sortalde occupies the *east-across-Hafra* slot, so the Empire's continent is **west across Hafra** or possibly a different bearing. Crossing takes long enough that landings on Talan are campaigns, not raids.
**What's open:** Name, internal geography, the Empire's borders within it, whether other polities exist alongside it (vassals, rivals, conquered remnants), exact crossing distance.
**Where it lives:** [lore/geography.md](../lore/geography.md) — *Other Continents*; [lore/factions.md](../lore/factions.md) — *Red Empire*.

### Eight remaining Nine Generals dungeons — geographic placement

## Factions & Politics

### The Divine Faith — pantheon identity
**Status:** high — the most politically significant non-bound god currently active on Talan.
**What's decided:** The Faith is the state religion of the **Legea Empire** (Zuzental sub-region). Its god is not bound; resides in **Layer 3 (Postlife)**. The Empire is ruled by a **demigod line**, with a current **theocratic prince** as heir. The Faith runs an active missionary programme; the Draconis Fenurran tribe has arranged a political marriage of their daughter to the theocratic prince.
**What's open:**
- Specific plane (Elysium, Diyu, or Abyss) and the god's name
- Name and nature of the demigod ruler
- Personal name of the theocratic prince
- The Faith's relationship to Forseti and the bound thirteen (tolerance, rivalry, active conflict)
- Whether the Legea Empire predates the Gods' Law or postdates the Week of Crimson Rain
- Doctrinal contrast: how the Faith's law differs from Forseti's, where they conflict in practice
**Where it lives:** [lore/factions.md](../lore/factions.md) — *Non-Bound God Faiths*; [lore/cultures.md](../lore/cultures.md) — *Open threads & TBD*.

### Kitsune religion
**Status:** medium — Heartcourt has nine pillars; faith is conspicuously not one of them.
**What's decided:** The Heartcourt's nine Hearts cover Military, Diplomacy, Commerce, Nature, Law, Magic, Arts, Knowledge, Secrets. No Faith pillar.
**What's open:** Whether kitsune worship one of the Thirteen (Fisaya is depicted as a kitsune — natural fit), ancestral fox-spirits, something else entirely, or operate as functional secularists.
**Where it lives:** [lore/cultures.md](../lore/cultures.md) — *Kitsune → Open threads & TBD*.

### Heartcourt — specific seated Hearts
