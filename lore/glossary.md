# Glossary: Coined Names & Etymologies

A working record of names in the setting. Per the naming convention in `CLAUDE.md`:
- **Old/ancient things** → Basque or Icelandic source, with linguistic drift
- **New/modern things** → plain English

When adding a new coined name, record: **Name · Source language · Literal meaning · Drift notes**.

### Cosmology / Planes
- **Tyrnarra**: the world
- **Talan**: the central continent
- **Solyra**: the golden sun. Also the name of the **Sun God** (Major God of the Sun, Diyu; the elder sibling of the celestial trio); the celestial body and the deity share a name. Full etymology and theological detail in *Named Non-Bound Gods (Layer 3 residents)* below.
- **Veyru**: the red moon. Also the **Dark Twin** of Bikiargi (the malevolent face of the lunar portfolio); celestial body and twin-half share a name. See *Named Non-Bound Gods (Layer 3 residents)* below for the full etymology and theological detail.
- **Unaru**: the green moon. Also the **Bright Twin** of Bikiargi (the benevolent face of the lunar portfolio); celestial body and twin-half share a name. See *Named Non-Bound Gods (Layer 3 residents)* below for the full etymology and theological detail.
- **The Cloud Sea**: the white vapor ocean surrounding the lands
- **The Wellspring**: origin/destination plane
- **Feyworld / Material Plane / Shadowplane**: the three planes of the Life layer
- **Elysium / Diyu / Abyss**: the three planes of the Postlife layer
- **The Aurora Veil** (Veil of Genesis): transitional veil at Prelife
- **The Duskmire** (Shroud of Return): transitional veil at Postlife
- **Dauria**: **Judgment City of the dead**, in Diyu. Souls arrive from the **Duskmire** (the Shroud of Return), are judged here, and are dispatched to their destination plane via the moon-cycle Wellspring channels. The cosmological router for the dead. *(Full canon in `geography/bolverk.md`: the Dauria-to-Bolverk routing.)*
- **Bolverk**: the megacity in **Abyss**; seat of the seven **Vice Demons** and the fourteen **Virtue Devils**.
  - **Source:** Icelandic *bölverkur* "evil-deed."
  - **Drift:** dropped the *-ur* nominative ending, then treated as a single-word place-name. *bölverkur* → *bölverk* → **Bolverk**.
  - **Full canon:** `geography/bolverk.md` (city geography, factions, soul-routing, individual seat-holders).
- **Tunsund**: the channel through Bolverk between the Vice-Demon and Virtue-Devil sides, carrying souls from Dauria via the daily Wellspring-flood.
  - **Source:** Icelandic *tunglsund* "moon-channel" (from *tungl* "moon" + *sund* "channel/strait"). The moons drive the flood, hence the name.
  - **Drift:** dropped the middle consonant cluster *gl*. *tunglsund* → **Tunsund**.
  - **Full canon:** `geography/bolverk.md`, *The Tunsund* (flood cycle, terraces, time-prestige).

### Vice Demons: Bolverk's seven seat-holders

The seats are immortal (as long as vice exists in mortals); the *holders* can be killed and replaced. The names below are the **current** holders. All seven coined from Icelandic + drift.

| Vice | Current Holder | Source | Drift |
|---|---|---|---|
| Pride | **Drambur** | Icelandic *drambast* "to be haughty, boast" | verb-root taken as a name, *-ur* name-ending added |
| Greed | **Nirfel** | Icelandic *nirfill* "miser" | dropped the final *l* |
| Lust | **Lostar** | Icelandic *losti* "lust" | added *-ar* name-ending |
| Envy | **Ofunda** | Icelandic *öfund* "envy" | vowel softened, *-a* feminine-name-ending added |
| Gluttony | **Veislur** | Icelandic *veisla* "feast" | re-stemmed with *-ur* ending |
| Wrath | **Reidar** | Icelandic *reiði* "wrath" | added *-ar* name-ending, hardened consonants |
| Sloth | **Lethar** | Icelandic *leti* "sloth" | inserted medial *-h-*, added *-ar* ending |

**Atezar**: Basque *atezain* ("gatekeeper, doorkeeper") → drift *atezain → atezar*. Minor god of the late Gods' Era; head of a small frontier pantheon (five gods, names lost), venerated by border garrisons and wall-towns. His pantheon and last followers were annihilated in the Week of Crimson Rain. At the forging of the Gods' Law he was sorted *dangerous* and banished to Layer 3; in Abyss he killed and replaced the Reidar of the day and has been the **current Reidar** since shortly after the Gods' Law was sealed. Full canon: `geography/bolverk.md`, *Reidar: the Wrath Vice Demon*.

**Lethar: Outer-being origin note.** Of the seven, Lethar is the only seat-holder who is neither a mortal soul nor a god of Tyrnarra. He is an **Outer being** from a multiversal reality beyond the Wellspring's reach, drawn into Abyss across the Astral when sloth first emerged as a recognised vice among mortals and the Sloth seat's calling reached outside Tyrnarra. He is the **original holder** of his seat (the only one of the seven who is) and has held it since it formed. Full canon: `geography/bolverk.md`, *Lethar: the Sloth Vice Demon*; multiversal-realities canon: `cosmology.md`, *Tyrnarra: The World & Sky*.

**Henrick Brodd**: plain English with light drift, fitting the Dark Era register. Forge Dwarf innkeeper of a small mining-hamlet in upper Brauogi during the Dark Era. Inherited a fragmentary cookbook he could not fully read; followed the diagrams; fed the village on what the diagrams produced for roughly seventy years. Died of (extended) old age c. early Adventurer Era; soul fell to Bolverk soaked in residue; took the Veislur seat shortly after arrival and has held it for about six hundred and fifty years. Current **Veislur**. Full canon: `geography/bolverk.md`, *Veislur: the Gluttony Vice Demon*.

**Captain Mara Goldwake**: plain English, Adventurer-Era pirate-byname register. Tengu Pirate Lord of the Twin Cities council across most of the mid-24th century MR. Built a forty-year career on raiding for things-to-bury and sailing for places-to-bury-them, countless individual caches scattered across every god domain on Talan (mortal counts disagree, from a few hundred to vastly more), all locations held in her head, nothing written down. Died c. 2390 MR at about seventy. Soul fell to Bolverk Greed-perfect; took the Nirfel seat on arrival by offering the previous holder a single cache as a gift (which carried the catalogue with it; the previous Nirfel burst from the impossibility). Current **Nirfel**, second-youngest seat-holder. Full canon: `geography/bolverk.md`, *Nirfel: the Greed Vice Demon*.

**Klumpa: Lostar's old alley-name.** Icelandic-rooted *klumpa* "lump." The contempt-name given to Lostar in her demonling years on Bolverk's lower-tier alleys, when she was a squat, asymmetrical demonborn with no obvious future. She has not responded to it in centuries, but some old demons in Bolverk still remember it. Of the seven, Lostar is the only **demonborn** seat-holder: never a mortal soul, never a god, native to Bolverk's substrate. She rose by a craft of her own invention: a deliberate study of how desire is produced in the eye of the wanter, distilled into repeatable technique. Current **Lostar**, holding the seat for about four hundred years. Full canon: `geography/bolverk.md`, *Lostar: the Lust Vice Demon*.

### Sub-regions (coined following the naming rule)

**Vindul (Wind):**
- **Haizetsua**: Basque *haize* (wind); island name echoes domain character
  - **Vindboorg**: Icelandic *vindr* (wind) + *borg* (fortress, town) → *Vindborg* → drift to *Vindboorg* "Wind-Hold." Drift: vowel-elongation through long use. Capital of the Skarvorn Republic; the most-named of the singing cities.
- **Baerfrost**: English-style, arctic northern sub-region. Home of the **Hunt-League** Jotunborn polity (clan chieftains chosen meritocratically, midwinter Gathering votes a one-year **Hammer-Lord**); a Dark-Era survival institution now under pressure from southern trade and rising border jarldoms. Full canon in `geography/vindul.md`, *Baerfrost*.
- **Air Monastery**: Plain English (modern stratum). Fortified mountain enclave in Vindul's eastern range, autonomous territory; the mother-house of **the Wyndwalken**. The sub-region name describes the place; the order's name is its own coinage (see *Faction proper nouns → The Wyndwalken*). Full canon in `geography/vindul.md`, *Air Monastery*.
  - **Hartzar Erruta** "The Star-Watched Crag": Basque *haitz* (rock, crag) + *izar* (star) → *haitz-izar* → drifted to **Hartzar** (consonant cluster smoothed, vowel-loss); + *errota* (mill, turning thing; archaic use for anything that turns by force of weather) → drifted to **Erruta** (loss of trailing *-o*, vowel-doubling for cadence). The mother-house complex itself, the named crag on which the Air Monastery is built. Deep-old stratum name: the rock was named before any order took it, and the modern Wyndwalken inherited the toponym.
- **Azkamour**: Basque *azkar* (swift, quick) + *muga* (border) → *azkar-muga* → drifted *azkamoua > azkamour* "the Swift March." Border territory between Vindul and Brauogi, **capital on the Vindul side**. The Earth-and-Wind crossroads; mostly golden Brauogi-character grain territory inland, but the Vindul coastal stretch anchors the capital where storm-fishing meets grain-roads. The name records that grain and storm-news both move fast through.
- **Fellibylur**: Icelandic *fell* (lone mountain, fell) + *bylur* (snowstorm, blizzard, gale) → *fellsbylur > Fellibylur* "Storm-Fell / Blizzard Peak." Drift: genitive *-s-* worn to *-i-*. The deep-old peak-reading faded over centuries; the name persists as a description of the region's character: open grass-steppe between **Baerfrost** (north, across the river that runs east through Villtur), **Azkamour** (southwest), **Brauogi** (south + east), and the **Hafra coast** (west). Storm-wracked weather year-round. **Strix**-anchored kingdom governed under the **Stormpact** (chartered Council of two seats: **the Skybell Republic** on the coast, **the Thunderhost** in the interior). Signature export: **Stormriders**, foul-weather Hafra pilots. Full canon in `geography/vindul.md`, *Fellibylur*.
  - **Brasswatch**: Plain English (modern stratum). *Brass* (the consecrated bell-metal) + *watch* (the tower-watch of the bell-network). Port-capital of Fellibylur, seat of the Skybell Republic on the Hafra coast.
  - **Cloudshadow**, **Highmarrow**, **Wrackmoor**: Plain English (modern stratum). Subsidiary Skybell ports of Fellibylur, each with its own consecrated bell-tower; all four ports (with Brasswatch) maintain the storm-warning network jointly.
  - **The Hightable**: Plain English (modern stratum). The fixed inland midsummer gathering-site of the Thunderhost; also the name of the high-summer storm-season the gathering opens. The season takes its name from the site; the site takes its name from the elevated flat where the Strix clan-chieftains have always perched to deliberate.

**Lautara (Commerce):**
- **Azkataria**: Basque *azoka* (market, fair) + *-tar(ra)* (people-of, native-of) → *azokataria* → vowel-loss to **Azkataria** "market-folk / people of the market." The agricultural feeder belt south of Merkavar, wrapping the southern face of the city-state's lake-and-Helgafjall territory and holding the southern approach to the sacred peak. The market-folk are the orchard-keepers, vintners, market-gardeners, and dairy-farmers whose work orbits Merkavar's market gravity, organised under the chartered League of Cellar Houses (the **Cellarsworn**; see *Faction proper nouns*). Vishkanya-anchored Cellar Master class, Halfling village-and-trail-keeper layer, cosmopolitan mix in the stewardry, Merkavar-kitsune information-stalls in the larger market-towns.
  - **Roskelstein**: Icelandic *Þröskuldssteinn* (threshold stone) → drift: initial *Þr-* softens to *R-*, genitive *-s-* worn, doubled *-ss-* smoothed → **Roskelstein** "Threshold Stone." The deep-old shrine at the trailhead where the pilgrim route up Helgafjall begins. The Council of Cellar Masters convenes here on the four-Azoka cadence. Threshold-tokens (small object-offerings each pilgrim sets down at the trailhead to mark what they hope to leave behind, bring back, or be changed by) left here by ascending pilgrims are gathered into the Pilgrim Houses' reckoning and disposed of by ritual.
  - **Fornbar**: Icelandic *fornn* (ancient) + *bær* (farm, estate) → *Fornbær* → vowel-smoothing → **Fornbar** "the Ancient Estate." The oldest Pilgrim House of the Cellarsworn; deepest cellar, longest trail-charter, prestige seat of the League.
  - **Lestastad**: Icelandic *lestur* (reading) + *staður* (place) → *Lestrastaður* → vowel-loss + trailing *-ur* dropped → **Lestastad** "the Reading-Place." The scholar's Pilgrim House of the Cellarsworn; largest cellar-library, standing **Helgaft** residency for Thekkavar scholars, the scholar-Master pipeline runs through here.
  - **Starnasal**: Icelandic *stjörnur* (stars) + *salur* (hall) → *Stjörnusalur* → drift through *Starnusalur* → **Starnasal** "Star-Hall." The astronomer's Pilgrim House of the Cellarsworn; oriented to the higher way-shrines, which natural philosophers use as observation-shelters at first dark.
  - **Goldenleaf**: Plain English (modern stratum). One of the post-Adventurer-Era Cellar Houses founded as a pure wine-and-orchard estate after the League charter; vineyards turn the colour of its name in the weeks before Uzta-Azoka.
  - **Sundwell**: Modern English + drift; *sun + well* with archaic *-d-* insertion (cf. *sund* in old English place-names). One of the post-Adventurer-Era Cellar Houses; holds the sun-rich south-facing slopes, the longest light, the warmest cellars.
  - **Highvine**: Plain English (modern stratum). One of the post-Adventurer-Era Cellar Houses; holds the upper-altitude vineyards toward the Helgafjall foothills, where the grape ripens slower and the cellar runs colder by a degree all year.
- **Emarrea**: Basque *ema* (soft, gentle, calm) + *arrea* (tawny, sandy-brown) → *ema-arrea* "the soft tawny [land]" → assimilated to **Emarrea**. The kitsune kingdom; fox-colour and gentle-host disposition encoded in the name. Forested western highlands of Lautara. Capital: Biozuri.
- **Biozuri**: Basque *bihotz* (heart) + *hiri* (city). *bihotz-hiri* → drop the medial h → *bihotzuri* → soften tz→z → **Biozuri** "heart-city." Hidden modern-Basque double-reading: *bio* (life-root) + *zuri* (white/pale) = "pale life," fitting the foxfire-lit shimmering valleys. Capital of Emarrea, seat of the Heartcourt.
- **Rika Tikur**: Icelandic *ríkur* (rich, wealthy) + *tígulegur* (splendid, stately) → **Rika Tikur** "Rich and Splendid." The compound is imperfect, less a clean Icelandic phrase than a boastful self-applied brag that stuck. An independent coastal island-city set inside the Order of Steam bay in Lautara, naming itself for the wealth it claims and handling the bulk industrial freight from the OoS volcanic-belt manufactories.
- **The Dreaming Cape**: Modern English (modern stratum). Substantial coastal sub-region of Lautara forming the north-east headland of the Order of Steam bay and wrapping around the bay's south and east coasts. Carries a phenomenon locals call **the drift**, a persistent dreaming sub-state of the local air read as Bikiargi's nearness by temple doctrine and as multiple folk-theories by everyone else (cause genuinely canon-pending at chronicler-tier; the GM-tier truth is a subsurface Stillpool-river, see `geography/lautara.md`, *Dreaming Cape → ⚿ GM Secret: The Dreaming Cape: The Real History*). Governed by the **Cape Theocracy of Bikiargi** (see *Faction proper nouns → The Twin Lantern*), a gynocratic theocracy headed by the paired identical-twin **Bright Voice** and **Dark Voice**. The dream-trade and the **Wakehelm** pilot-craft are the polity's signature exports. Capital: **Moonwatch**.
  - **Moonwatch**: Plain English (modern stratum). The Cape's capital and temple-city, on the north-east headland at the bay-mouth where the Twin Lantern temple-tower doubles as the bay-mouth lighthouse. The city watches the moon (Bikiargi) and the bay-traffic at once.
  - **The Twin Lantern**: Modern English (modern stratum). The high temple of Bikiargi at Moonwatch; the temple-tower IS the lighthouse, the lighthouse IS the temple-tower. *Twin* reads the double-meaning of the twin moons and the twin Voices; *lantern* is the lighthouse-flame and the goddess's light. Long-form: *the Twin Lantern of Bikiargi*. The polity's name as well: see *Faction proper nouns → The Twin Lantern*.
  - **Markaa**: Icelandic *markaá* (border-river) → *mark* (border) + *á* (river) → drift: doubled *-aá* smoothed to **Markaa**. The major river forming the Cape/Tahu Tangata border in its western/northern stretch, continuing east as the Tahu Tangata/Emarrea and Itsasalda/Emarrea borders, eventually reaching Merkavar's lake. Norse-stratum name matching Helgafjall's register; the Talanese-canonical name used across all sub-regions on its course (no local cultural variants in current canon).
  - **The Silver Light**: Modern English (modern stratum). The founding inn of Moonwatch; the oldest dream-house, where the dream-blend trade first organised under temple license. The lay reflection of the Twin Lantern: the inn that catches the lighthouse-flame from down at the harbour.
- **Itsasalda**: Basque *itsasoaldea* "the seaside region" (*itsaso* "sea" + *aldea* "region") → vowel-loss to **Itsasalda** "seaside." Substantial central-northern sub-region of Lautara with the **Dreaming Cape** as its NW neighbour across **Vordfjall** and **Smáa**, **Atarialda** to the E, **Azkataria** to the SE, **Merkavar** to the S, and **Emarrea** to the SW across the **Markaa**. Halfling-dominated population through every working layer (rail, river, coast, dock); governed by **the Conductor-Magistracy** (see *Faction proper nouns*), a distributed circuit-Magistrate polity with no fixed seat, fed by a formal HRA promotion pipeline. De-facto capital: **Bellhalt**. Holds Itsasalda's half of the partitioned island of **Hringseyja** (bisected with the Legea Empire across **the Quietline**).
  - **Bellhalt**: Plain English (modern stratum); compound *bell* (the Halfling signal-bell tradition + the rail-stop bells) + *halt* (rail-halt). The de-facto capital of Itsasalda; rail-halt, principal river, and Midarra coast meet at the largest Muino-saila gap. Holds the HRA's Itsasalda regional headquarters and the Senior Magistrate's correspondence address.
  - **Hringseyja**: Icelandic *hringr* (ring, circle) + *eyja* (island) → minimal drift → **Hringseyja** "Ring Island." Circular island offshore in Midarra, bisected between Itsasalda (Lautara) and the Legea Empire (Zuzental) along **the Quietline**. Named for its enduring shape; the partition is recent in the island's geological-deep timeframe.
  - **The Quietline**: Modern English (modern stratum). The fixed partition line bisecting Hringseyja between Itsasalda and the Legea Empire. Sworn in a named year (canon-pending) of the mid-Adventurer Era between the Senior Magistrate of the day and the Empire's regional governor. Folk-shorthand on partition-side villages: **the Edge**. Cyprus-pattern uneasy coexistence: not contested but not friendly, formal embassies across by announced protocol only.
  - **Smáa**: Icelandic *smáá* "small river" → drift smooths doubled *-áá* to *-áa* → **Smáa**. The smaller river forming part of the Cape/Itsasalda border, running alongside the **Vordfjall** range. Norse-stratum parallel to Markaa.
  - **Vordfjall**: Icelandic *vörð-fjall* "watch-mountain" → drift smooths consonant cluster *-rðf-* to *-rdf-* → **Vordfjall**. The mountain range between the Dreaming Cape (west) and Itsasalda (east). The name carries a watch-meaning that predates current usage; a mountain monastery or fortress sits on its slopes, character canon-pending.
  - **Muino-saila**: Basque *muino* (hill) + *saila* (range, line); minimal drift, hyphen preserved → **Muino-saila** "the hill-range." The low coastal range along Itsasalda's northern Midarra coast, partially blocking direct coast access; the region's port-towns sit in the gaps.
- **Atarialda**: Basque *atari* (threshold, entrance, gateway) + *aldea* (region) → *atarialdea* → vowel-loss to **Atarialda** "the threshold-region." Eastern arm of Lautara, holding the overland trade routes toward Egulon, Ezkudon, and Zuzental. The gateway-region: caravans leaving Merkavar east cross Atarialda, and Crossroads sits at its northeast corner as the literal *atari* of the domain.
- **Helgafjall**: Icelandic *helgr* (holy, sacred) + *fjall* (mountain) → **Helgafjall** "holy mountain" (near-undrifted). The sacred snow-capped peak that rises from the centre of Merkavar's lake. Sacred-mountain watcher over the market-city; pilgrim trails wind up its slopes, the snow on its peak never fully melts, the lake at its foot has never frozen in record. Norse-stratum balance against the Basque-heavy Lautara sub-region naming.

**Myrkono (Darkness):**
- **Ilun Tasun**: Basque *ilun* (dark) + *tasun* (-ness suffix) = "the darkness itself"
- **Itzasoa**: echoes Basque *itsasoa* (sea/ocean); coastal sub-region
- **Bikitsa**: Basque *biki* (twin) + *itzal* (shadow) → *biki-itzal* → consonant assimilation and final-syllable drop → **Bikitsa** "twin-shadow." The coastal sub-region at the northern edge of Myrkono, sharing the two-sun phenomenon with the Brauogi-side **Twin Suns** (see Brauogi → Twin Suns); jointly governed with Twin Suns under a single shared structure (form canon-pending). Where Brauogi names what the two suns bring (light, harvest, gold), Myrkono names what they cast (twin shadows on the same coast). The cross-domain governance is unique on Talan and the closest the bound thirteen have come to permitting a polity that answers to two domains at once.

**Floteyn (Water):**
- **Balatur Erui**: Basque *balea* (whale) + Icelandic *eyri* (sandbank, sea-spit of land) → *balea-eyri* → drifted to **Balatur Erui** "whale-spit / whale shoals." Hybrid Basque/Icelandic, echoing the Burdineyja pattern. Drifting islands and whales together fit Floteyn double; Balaena the Skywhale City sits within the same archipelago.

**Sumendar (Fire):**
- **Burdineyja**: Basque *burdin* (iron/metal) + Icelandic *eyja* (islands) = Iron Islands
- **Haraour Eliza**: Basque *eliza* (church); possibly religious sub-region
- **Tahu Tangata**: indigenous culture name, meaning partially lost
- **No Man's Land**: modern English; Komo's city-state territory
- **Order of Steam**: modern English; industrial kingdom-guild hybrid
- **Dragon's Reach**: modern English; dragon civilization capital

**Lioaru (Time):**
- **Galdua Jendea**: Basque; meaning disputed ("Place of Many Waters" or "Place of the Lost People")
- **Lost Kingdom**: modern English; temporally unstable ruins
- **River Duchies**: modern English; oasis river valley confederation
- **Hareaveldi**: Basque *harea* (sand) + Icelandic *veldi* (realm, dominion) → **Hareaveldi** "Sand Realm." Hybrid Basque/Icelandic; minimal drift, initial *h-* aspiration lost only, both roots otherwise intact. The deep-old name a pre-Tani people gave the dune country, which was already a realm of its own when Tani arrived in Lioaru. Partially independent cultural identity persists.

**Brauogi (Earth):**
- **Gotorlekua**: Basque *gotor* (strong, firm, sturdy) + *leku* (place) + *-a* (definite article) → **Gotorlekua** "the Stronghold." Near-undrifted Basque; the name reads younger than its referent suggests, either because the meaning has refreshed it in usage over the millennia, or because plain compounds erode less than poetic ones. Houses Lurrath, Sarrum's city-state (Steadfast City), set within the ring of peaks called **Eraztumen** that gives the sub-region its Stronghold name in fact.
- **The Earth Realm**: English-style. The agricultural heart of Brauogi between Iturmen (north) and Mugamen (south). Fully golden plains — grain, livestock, market-towns. Hirubaso sits as an enclave in its south-centre but is its own independent sub-region (see below).
- **Baratalda**: Basque *baratze* (garden, orchard) + *aldea* (region) → *baratze-aldea* → vowel-loss and drift drops the medial *z* → **Baratalda** "the orchard-region." Eastern arm of Brauogi at the Ehizahar border; mixed golden plain and green orchard country. The orchard-and-fruit-cellar of the domain; harvest-fairs draw Ehizahar hunters down from the Lands of Villtur to trade game and furs for wine, apple-brandy, and stone-fruit. The frontier-trade kingdom of the bread-domain.
- **Twin Suns**: modern English. Coastal sub-region of Brauogi on the Midarra-touching corner; the Brauogi-side half of the **Twin Suns / Bikitsa** border kingdom shared with Myrkono. Golden, food-rich coast where the two-sun phenomenon is observed at certain seasons. The Myrkono-side counterpart (**Bikitsa**, see Myrkono section) sees the same phenomenon from its shadow-domain side; the two halves are jointly governed under a single shared structure (form canon-pending). The specific phenomenon that earned the "twin suns" framing is an open thread (see `docs/open-threads.md`).
- **Soul Tree**: modern English. A small rocky island in Midarra, just off the coast of Twin Suns in Brauogi. A natural soul-passage point to the Postlife; the **Voroir Daua's oldest charter** sits directly on the island. Earth-domain affiliation reads as cosmologically apt: a great rooted tree through which souls pass is Sarrum-the-Steadfast iconography.
- **Iturmen**: Basque *iturri* (spring, source) + *mendi* (mountain) → *iturri-mendi* → vowel-loss to **Iturmen** "source-mountain." The central east-west mountain spine of Brauogi; the headwaters of most of the domain's rivers spring from here. Habitable in places; pass-towns hold the spine where the trade-roads cross.
- **Iturburu**: Basque *iturri* (spring, source) + *buru* (head) → *iturri-buru* → vowel-loss to **Iturburu** "source-head, the headwaters." The great mountain-lake under Iturmen's tallest peak; the practical mother-lake of the domain's rivers. Where Lautara has the sacred Helgafjall above Merkavar, Brauogi has the livelihood Iturburu — the lake whose feeders fill every farmer's irrigation channel. Mythic vs. agricultural; pilgrim's mountain vs. miller's mountain.
- **Hirubaso**: Basque *hiru* (three) + *baso* (forest) → drifted from longer *hiru-ibai-baso* "three-river-forest" with loss of medial *ibai* → **Hirubaso**. The sacred druid grove of Sarrum at the centre of the Earth Realm, where three rivers converge in the open plains. Tended by an order of Sarrum's faithful; the still point of the breadbasket, the wild left untouched at the heart of the cultivated world. **The only routine Primotech site on the continent**, where Brauogi's legendary mineral-Magitech tradition (`cosmology.md`) finds its grown-not-built source; the rumoured Brauogi heart-stone is grown here.
- **Eraztumen**: Basque *eraztun* (ring) + *mendi* (mountain) → *eraztun-mendi* → consonant assimilation + vowel-loss to **Eraztumen** "ring-mountain." The ring of peaks around Gotorlekua and Lurrath; the geographic feature that gives the Stronghold its name. The inside of the ring is Gotorlekua proper; the ring itself is the natural border of the sub-region.
- **Mugamen**: Basque *muga* (border) + *mendi* (mountain) → *muga-mendi* → assimilation to **Mugamen** "border-mountain." The southern mountain range that walls Brauogi off from Myrkono; the line where the bread country ends and the deep forests of Araphel's domain begin. Brauogi-side farmers and Myrkono-side shadow-folk both look at the same wall from opposite sides.

**Ezkudon (Knowledge):**
- **Jakinduria**: Basque *jakinduria* (wisdom/knowledge); real word, no drift required

**Egulon (Light):**

Trade-nexus settlement on the southern tri-domain border where Zuzental, Lautara (Commerce), and Egulon (Light) meet. **Functionally independent** of all three; nominally a Zuzental sub-region by the geography of the tri-point but answers to no domain's law. ### Fenurran proper nouns (Latinate / English register, not the deep-old stratum)

The Fenurran culture is detailed in `geography/ehizahar/fenurra.md`. Its internal place- and weapon-names sit in a Latinate / Germanic / English register, not the deep Basque/Icelandic stratum. They function as "modern" coined names in the world (the Fenurran people are old, but their *naming language* is closer to plain-style than the deep-old domains).

- **Aeris**: Fenurran capital, carved into the inner walls of the Scar of Aeris. Latinate stylization (Latin *aer/aeris* = "of the air"). Etymology partial; Fenurran-internal name.

### Kitsune proper nouns (Japanese-style internal register)

The kitsune culture is detailed in `geography/lautara/emarrea.md`. Outsiders refer to their kingdom and capital by the old-language names (Emarrea, Biozuri, listed under Lautara sub-regions above). Internally, the kitsune use a Japanese-flavoured cultural register for their own places, dishes, rituals, and titles. Same pattern as Fenurra's internal Latinate vocabulary.

**Language:**
- **Kotokoe**: *koto* (word, thing, sound) + *koe* (voice) → "the voice of words." The kitsune cultural tongue, used internally in Emarrea alongside Talanese (the continental common). Names of places, dishes, rituals, and gods within the kingdom are coined in Kotokoe; outside the kingdom, trade and diplomacy run in Talanese. Most kitsune are fluent in both from childhood. Japanese-flavoured register, drifted across the kingdom's history.

**Pantheon (the Kyūbi-no-Den, the kitsune ancestral pantheon):**
- **Kyūbi-no-Den**: *kyūbi* (nine-tailed) + *den* (hall) → "the Nine-Tailed Hall." Name of the kitsune's own ancestral pantheon. Three named gods sit it (Hahane, Honokage, Yumegatari); the kitsune say the other six seats wait on ancestral spirits, place-spirits, or stories not yet long enough to fill them. The number nine echoes the nine-tail mastery ladder and the Heartcourt's nine pillars. Co-exists with worship of the Bound Thirteen (especially Fisaya, who is depicted in kitsune iconography as a kitsune); kitsune religion is plural and open. Full canon in `gods.md`, *Other Pantheons → The Kyūbi-no-Den*.
- **Hahane**: *haha* (mother) + *ne* (root, source) → "the Mother-Root" / "the Rooted Mother." Coined in Kotokoe. **Minor Goddess** of love, polyamory, family, festival, and the polycule bond. Resides in **Elysium** (Layer 3, good). Head of the Kyūbi-no-Den. The Passing of the Tails ritual invokes her; first incense at every household shrine is hers. The Heartcourt's *Rose Heart* (Arts & Festivals) often serves as her informal high-priest. **Cleric domain grants:** Family · Passion · Indulgence.
- **Honokage**: *hono* (faint, soft, the first flicker) + *kage* (light-play, shadow) → "the Soft-Shadow," the god glimpsed in lantern-light but never seen straight. **Minor God** of foxfire, illusion, kind misdirection, and the lantern-lit night. Resides in **Elysium** (Layer 3, good). Patron of Occult practitioners across the kingdom (foxfire being the kitsune signature of the Occult tradition; see `geography/lautara/emarrea.md`, *Foxfire*). Receives foxfire-offerings rather than incense; lanterns are lit *for* him as much as *by* him. **Cleric domain grants:** Trickery · Magic · Moon (the lantern-bearer face of the lunar portfolio, co-granted alongside Bikiargi).
- **Yumegatari**: *yume* (dream) + *katari* (telling, narration) → "the Dream-Teller." **Minor Goddess** of story, memory, wish-lanterns, and legacy. Resides in **Elysium** (Layer 3, good). The wish-lanterns floated down the Hoshigawa at Kawaakari are her offering; the **Storyweaver** at the House of a Thousand Flavors is by ancient courtesy understood to speak for her at the table. **Cleric domain grants:** Dreams · Knowledge · Soul (memory-as-Wellspring-imprint).

**Magic tradition:**
- **Foxfire**: pale gold-and-blue illusion-fire that throws no real heat, the kitsune signature of **Occult magic** (see `cosmology.md` → *Magic, The Four Schools*). Used in lanterns, illusion-craft, performance, the Foxfire Stone Massage, the wish-lanterns of Kawaakari, and the course-illusions at the House of a Thousand Flavors. Patronised by **Honokage** in the Kyūbi-no-Den. Not exclusive to kitsune (any Occult caster can produce the same effect), but *characteristic* of them.

**Places (kitsune-internal):**
- **Heartplaza**: central district of Biozuri, the bustling civic heart of the capital. Modern English compound.
- **Kawaakari**: "river light." The mountain-pass resort village famous for hot springs, the shimmering Hoshigawa river, and the Foxfire Stone Massage. Japanese-internal naming.
- **Hoshigawa**: "star river." The crystal-fed mountain river flowing through Kawaakari; trace crystal dust suspended in the water sparkles even by day. Japanese-internal naming.

**Government:**
- **The Heartcourt**: Plain English. The ruling polycule of 4-9 Hearts governing Emarrea.
- **The nine Hearts**: Plain English. Each Heart oversees one of the nine great pillars (Crimson/Military, Azure/Diplomacy, Golden/Commerce, Emerald/Nature, Ivory/Law, Violet/Magic, Rose/Arts, Sapphire/Knowledge, Onyx/Secrets).

### Tengu proper nouns (Tengu cultural register)

The Tengu culture is detailed in `geography/vindul/haizetsua.md`. Outsiders refer to the island and capital by the old-language names (Haizetsua, Vindboorg, listed under *Vindul (Wind)* above). Internally, the Tengu use a distinct **Tengu cultural register** for their own institutions, clans, customs, and crafts: austere, consonant-heavy, hard finals, the *Tessek* family of sound. The register is a pure coinage stratum, not a translatable language; the etymologies below are coinage-notes, not interlinear gloss.

**Republic:**
- **Skarvorn**: Tengu-register coinage; *skar* (flock, multitude) + *vorn* (wing, voice) → "Flock-Voice." The seated body of Haizetsua's republic; the eleven clan-elders convene as the Skarvorn. No single ruler. Convenes quarterly at the **Open Pavilion** in Vindboorg.
- **The Open Pavilion**: Plain English. The Skarvorn's seating-place in Vindboorg.

**The eleven clans** (clan-and-monastery-and-trading-guild kindreds; each sends one elder to the Skarvorn):
- **Tessek**: Golivander Tessek's clan; most-known abroad through the chronicler's career
- **Karrok**
- **Vorrek**
- **Drennok**
- **Brakkar**
- **Nottek**
- **Vellok**
- **Holkar**
- **Marskenn**
- **Skenrak**
- **Drakkun**

(Individual clan-colours are unwritten by design; the structural equality between the eleven is the point. Future canon will shade them in as stories want.)

**Customs:**
- **Vornsketta**: Tengu-register coinage; *vorn* (wing) + *sketta* (journey, road-going) → "wing-journey." The migration custom: every Tengu in their first century leaves Haizetsua for three to twenty years and lives, works, and learns abroad. Older than the Republic. Golivander's seventy-year continent-wandering is the canonical model of a Vornsketta that became a vocation.
- **Founder-remembrance**: Plain English. The clan-internal practice of reciting first-generation names at clan-elder turning. Ancestor-respect, not ancestor-worship; the clans do not invoke their founders for power.

**Crafts:**
- **Air-carving**: Plain English. The mason-instrument-maker's craft that tunes Tengu dwellings to produce sustained musical tones when wind passes through their carved channels and cavities. A Tengu air-carver may train twenty years to tune a new house. The body of work the singing-cities tradition rests on.
- **Stormsinging**: Plain English. The Tengu vocal craft of singing *with* the city: pitching voice into the building's tones, projected through wind currents. Acoustic-and-breath discipline in ordinary practice; Occult-adjacent in skilled hands (cultural-memory residue, same mechanism as kitsune foxfire). **Thousand Kingdom: the noble-naming convention:**

Full canon in `geography/zuzental.md`, *Thousand Kingdom → Founding history*. The convention itself is **ancient**, older than the unified Thousand, older than House Fyrst itself; the small polities Renauld eventually unified each already kept registers in this form. Rumour holds it predates even Renauld's birth, though no Adventurer-Era scholar can demonstrate the claim. The system survived the Dark Era fracture: each fragment-kingdom kept registering, and at the 2457 MR re-founding Aelis Fyrstgilt did not invent the system, she merely re-took an old throne within it.

- **Form.** Every noble surname is **[house prefix] + [ancestry suffix]**. House prefix is the dynastic identifier (Raven, Bel, Fyrst). Ancestry suffix is determined by the *individual's* ancestry, not the house's, so one household can produce siblings with different surnames if the children are different ancestries.
- **Suffix at birth.** The herald-clerk records the suffix on the birth-register the day the child enters the world.
- **Heir-prefix rule.** One parent is the designated **head of family** (matriarch or patriarch; gender, biology, and adoption are all irrelevant to the designation; only registration matters). The **main heir** carries the head's prefix; **non-heir children** carry the secondary parent's prefix. Everyone's suffix tracks their own ancestry.
- **Heir-status mobility.** If the heir is replaced or disinherited, their **prefix changes**; they shift from the head's prefix to the secondary parent's prefix. Your legal surname literally rewrites when your position in the inheritance shifts. The country's most distinctive legal artefact: a disinherited noble walks out of the hall with a different surname than the one they walked in with.
- **Bastard override.** A recognised bastard of any ancestry bears the suffix **-born** *instead of* their ancestry-suffix. Bastardy is the dominant marker; ancestry is overridden. Unrecognised bastards bear commoner names only.
- **Royal elevation.** When a non-noble is elevated to the throne (as Aelis was), they **drop their commoner surname** and take the royal house prefix with their own ancestry suffix attached. Patronymic / matronymic middle is preserved.
- **Scope.** Thousand Kingdom only. The Emerald Isles, Legea Empire, Order of Law, and Namur Republic each run their own conventions. This system is one of the Thousand's most-formalised Forseti-domain artefacts and a quiet point of pride.

**Thousand Kingdom: commoner naming:**

Commoners run a **three-name structure** by social default: *given + middle + family*. The middle name is matronymic or patronymic, the named parent's given name. The three-name form is a low-grade snobbery; in a country whose pre-unification history was a thousand small kingdoms full of pretend-nobility, commoners assert their parental lineage to flag that they too have a parent worth naming. **Two-name commoners** (just given + family) read as too rural to bother with the affectation, too principled to imitate nobility, or unwilling to claim a parent (orphan, foundling, deliberately reticent). Register is light Germanic / French: given names like *Aldwin, Bren, Edda, Greta, Halver, Iona, Jacques, Konrad, Marien, Marthe, Mira, Pieter, Renier, Solène, Tomas, Wenzel*; surnames like *Halder, Brask, Trent, Vaughn, Mero, Vesh, Marche, Rouland, Schein, Halt, Bourdin, Karth, Velder*.

The gynocratic principle is most visible here: men serve as senior clergy in many capacities, but a Midnight Sister's seat is held by women only.
- **The Lunarcall**: Modern English (modern stratum). The ritual by which a new Bright Voice and Dark Voice are selected. Conducted by the Midnight Sisters whenever the Voices' seats fall vacant (which is always together: a surviving Voice and her dead sister cannot continue alone). The ritual reveals the twin-pair that dreams most vividly under Bikiargi's calling; candidates may come from any background (clergy ranks, established Cape families, refugees, anyone); the chosen pair are brought into novitiate together. (GM-tier: the ritual is a creation event, not a selection; see `geography/lautara.md`, *Dreaming Cape → ⚿ GM Secret*.)
- **The Wakehelm**: Modern English. The Twin Lantern's pilot-craft. A **paired** unit of one ship-pilot (lay) and one dream-pilot (clergy of Bikiargi), trained together at the temple's navigation-school and bearing the temple's joint mark. A ship that hires one hires both. **Midarra experts**: bay-mouth currents, two-headland approach geometry, Rika Tikur's heavy traffic, the routes south down the Sumendar Midarra-coast and east along Lautara's. Hafra crossings belong to other pilots (Fellibylur's Stormriders for cloud-sea, Floteyn's coastal craft for Hafra-coast); the Wakehelm leaves them alone. They navigate **through the drift**: a captain whose helm-orders arrive with a fractional delay, or a lookout who sees the next minute before this one, makes wrong calls without a Cape-trained pair in the bridge. The Wakehelm's continental reputation rests on this.
- **The dream-trade tiers**: The Cape's commercial offering, structured in five tiers by doctrine. (1) Free temple witnessing of dreams in the Twin Lantern dormitory. (2) Paid clergy interpretation of dreams brought to the temple. (3) Paid inn lodging at chartered inns. (4) Paid temple-stamped dream-blends at chartered inns (the brew is sold; the dream is the sleeper's). (5) Paid temple-monopoly prophetic induction; only Twin Lantern clergy may conduct it; the dream is intentionally produced for a client, but interpretation stays open and the answer stays dream-quality. **Doctrinal core**: the dream owned by a sleeper is sacred, and a dream sold to a stranger is a desecration. Inn-keepers who try to broker dream-content lose their licence.
- **The temple's stamp**: the temple-certification mark applied to dream-blends produced at chartered inns. The stamp certifies the brew, not the dream that follows. Inn-keepers brew under temple supervision (purity rules around ingredients and method); chartered inns serving unstamped brews lose their licence; unstamped brews exist outside the chartered system and are at-your-own-risk.

**The Conductor-Magistracy (Halfling-led polity of Itsasalda):**
- **The Conductor-Magistracy**: Modern English (modern stratum); compound *conductor* (the HRA's rail-and-craft conductor) + *magistracy* (the seated body of magistrates). The polity of Itsasalda, a distributed institutional polity with **no fixed seat**: five seated Magistrates ride portfolio circuits across the coast, rivers, rail, customs, and foreign-affairs work, holding court while they travel and corresponding between beats. Founded mid-Adventurer Era; the Magistracy formalised when the Legea Empire's missionary push onto Hringseyja required a polity with diplomatic standing to negotiate the partition. Halfling-led: structurally fed by the **Halfling Railworkers' Association** along a fixed promotion pipeline. Full canon in `geography/lautara.md`, *Itsasalda*.
- **Magistrate**: The working title for a seated member of the Conductor-Magistracy. Official documents use the portfolio prefix as the full title (e.g., **Foreign Magistrate**, **Senior Magistrate**, **River Magistrate**); *Magistrate* alone is the working-speech form. Each Magistrate holds a portfolio circuit and rides her beat; the Magistracy has no fixed seat.
- **The five Magistrate portfolios**: **Senior Magistrate** (peer-elected, first among equals); **Foreign Magistrate** (Hringseyja partition + wider Itsasalda foreign-affairs + sea-travel commerce; the only Magistrate authorised to leave the continent by sea on duty); **Coast Magistrate** (Muino-saila port-gaps + coast-customs); **River Magistrate** (inland river-routes to Merkavar); **Rail Magistrate** (Itsasalda rail-spur + HRA-labour-side relations).
- **The HRA pipeline**: The formal promotion path into the Magistracy: HRA member → Conductor → Senior Conductor → Magistrate Candidate → seated Magistrate. The Conductor-Magistracy is structurally the HRA's promotion ceiling. **Candidacy is continental**: any senior and trusted HRA member from any branch may stand. In practice most seated Magistrates come up through the Itsasaldan branch (proximity, familiarity), but cross-branch presence on the seated body is normal and is one of the things that keeps the HRA continental rather than regional. The non-HRA pipeline (river-captains, port-keepers) is closed by current standing rule; it is a live political tension.
- **Halfling Railworkers' Association (HRA)**: Modern English (modern stratum); plain compound. The continental labour organisation of Halfling rail-workers; the largest single voting bloc at Merkavar's autumn Uzta-Azoka assembly. **Two-layer institution**: labour (the working union, continental in reach) and governance (the seated Conductor-Magistracy in Itsasalda; the Magistracy is the Association's promotion ceiling). This two-layer character is unique on Talan and is what makes the HRA the most powerful Halfling institution on the continent. Full canon for the Merkavar voting role in `geography/lautara.md`, *Merkavar*; full canon for the Itsasalda governance role in `geography/lautara.md`, *Itsasalda*.

### Technology & Infrastructure

Full canon in `cosmology.md`, *Technology: Magitech and Infrastructure*.

- **Magitech**: Plain English (modern). **Umbrella term** for any applied integration of magic with material engineering on Talan. Not a fifth school of magic; an applied *integration* of the four existing schools. Has four sub-traditions (one per school): **Arcanotech, Occultech, Divitech, Primotech.** Common-place infrastructure across the continent; the Order of Steam (Sumendar) is the largest producer. **When mortals say "Magitech" without qualifying, they mean Arcanotech**, the same way "phone" defaults to "smartphone."
- **Arcanotech**: Plain English (modern, formed *Arcane + tech*). **By far the most common Magitech.** Arcane-school applied engineering: rune-arrays, formula-driven enchantment, reproducible manufacture. All Magitrains, almost all airships, the Order of Steam catalogue, streetlight grids, sanitation arrays, mercantile reckoning machines: Arcanotech. The natural industrial substrate because Arcane is formula-based, learnable, and scalable.
- **Occultech**: Plain English (modern, formed *Occult + tech*). **Rarer than Arcanotech.** Occult-school applied engineering: story-and-belief encoded into the object. Items are partly unique (the same blueprint produces different results in different cultural contexts) and depend on cultural memory to function. **Meaningfully more resilient than Arcanotech in chaos-disrupted regions** (Basogur, Blackened Lands, Darklands); Occult is narrative-based, not formula-based, so chaos-magic disruption hits it less. Occultech specialists are accordingly valuable for Adventurers' Guild cursed-region deployment.
- **Divitech**: Plain English (modern, formed *Divine + tech*). **Almost non-existent.** Divine-school applied engineering needs a god's continuing grant to function, which makes the item dependent on the relationship, not on itself. A few ceremonial / institutional pieces exist (a Wardstone-adjacent device in Myrria, Iro-sanctified hospital wards in Ljosarn) but Divitech does not scale: gods don't license their power for industrial production.
- **Primotech**: Plain English (modern, formed *Primal + tech*). **Essentially non-existent.** Primal magic *grows* rather than *manufactures*. The handful of canonically-extant Primotech items are all **legendary** (a living-wood polearm that bonds with its wielder across decades, leshy-grown bowyards, a rumoured Brauogi heart-stone). Most mortals will never see one in their lifetime.
- **Magitrain**: Plain English (modern, lightly drifted from *magic train*). The everyday name for the Arcanotech rail vehicles that run the continental network. **An Order of Steam invention** at the end of the Dark Era, out of the Order's concentrated Elden-tech excavation in the volcanic belt: the Lost-Era kingdoms and the Golden Empire had the underlying Arcanotech principles, but the working industrial engine and the long-line rail network are the Order's work. Two interconnected networks (Northern Talan, Southern Talan) cover most of the continent; the **Great Jungle (Basogur)** prevents through-rail between them. The Order remains the principal manufacturer of rolling stock on both halves.
- **Elden tech**: Plain English. The actual technological source for modern Arcanotech. **The Elden were a high-tech civilisation** (6000–2945 GR) before fusing with the Devourer to become the Corrupted God; their ruins yielded the rail, atmospheric, and industrial foundations modern Magitech evolved from. **Ultra-High-Tech Civilisation (UHTC)** is the folk-term mortals sometimes use without knowing the source was the Elden; scholarly usage just says *Elden tech* or *Elden-origin*. **No longer an open thread.** Full canon in `timeline.md`, *Elden Era → ⚿ GM Secret: The Elden: What Happened*, and `cosmology.md`, *Technology: Magitech and Infrastructure*.
- **Ship**: Plain English. **Normal wooden ships** still dominate Talanese waters by count. **Magitech ships** are the wealth upgrade, typically Arcanotech (rune-reinforced hulls, faster, larger, navigation aids), but **Occultech ships** exist as a chaos-water specialty: the Occult-resilience principle (narrative-based magic isn't disrupted by chaos magic) makes Occultech vessels the right choice for the cursed waters near the Blackened Lands, certain strange Floteyn deep currents, and the stormy outer ring of Sortalde. The Adventurers' Guild maintains a small Occultech sea fleet for cursed-region deployment.
- **Airship**: Plain English. **Always Magitech**; there are no non-magical airships; flight requires the magic. Two construction traditions: **Arcanotech airships** (rune-array lift, engine-driven propulsion, Order of Steam standard) and **Occultech airships** (each carries a **bound air elemental** as both lift-source and pilot-collaborator; the elemental cooperates, sometimes resists; the relationship is genuine, not a tool). Occultech airships are slower in fair weather but fly cleanly through chaos-magic regions where Arcanotech airships suffer. Vindul produces both hull types; the elementals come from Vindul's wind-summoner tradition.
- **Cloudship**: Plain English. **Very rare** specialist craft engineered for the **Cloud Sea**. **Every cloudship is dual-school Magitech**; Arcanotech alone cannot sustain the cross-veil transit the Cloud Sea demands. Three combinations exist: **Arcanotech + Occultech** (most working cloudships; Occultech provides the narrative-binding that lets the vessel "remember the way" across the formless Cloud Sea), **Arcanotech + Divitech** (god-sanctioned vessels: Adventurers' Guild, church of Iro, church of Fisaya have one or two each; vanishingly rare), and **Arcanotech + Primotech** (legendary living-hull seed-grown vessels: *Eyrasunda* is the famous one; most scholars believe such craft are folklore). The dual-school construction requirement is the structural reason cloudships are rare: you need cooperating craftsmen from two distinct Magitech traditions on the same hull, which is hard given how differently the schools operate. Cloudships also serve as luxury water vessels for the very wealthy, but their commercial purpose is the Cloud Sea crossing: the only practical way to reach Sortalde, the Red Empire's homeland, or any continent beyond Hafra.
- **The Continental Rail Network**: Plain English. Two interconnected halves, no through-rail across Basogur. Northern + Southern networks.
- **Train Pirates**: Plain English. Contemporary bandit category. Hit Magitrains at the slow stretches. Axiom: *Langsam auf Land, schnell auf Schiene* ("slow on land, fast on rail"). Most are mortal banditry; politically-motivated cells include Cronus-aligned freebooters and anti-industrial primal-aligned strikes.
- **The Conductor's Station**: Plain English (folk name). A rare pocket-dimension hazard of the Magitrain network: occasional derail into a starry-purple void where dropped objects and orphaned train cars drift. The five-eyed **Conductor** presides; his opening *"Tickets please!"* is a demand for memory, riddle, or a possession of personal significance. Get it right, return to ordinary tunnel-darkness; get it wrong, become part of the Lost and Found. **Haunts both networks equally**; no Northern or Southern bias.
- **Eyrasunda**: Icelandic *eyrar* "sandbanks, sandy shores" + *sunda* "sounds, narrow straits" → drifted to **Eyrasunda** "the in-between places." Legendary Arcanotech + Primotech **living-hull cloudship** of Vindul folklore: a single immense living tree grown into a cloudship hull, capable of self-repair across centuries. Whether *Eyrasunda* still exists is doctrinally argued in Vindul; most scholars believe she is folklore. The name encodes the cloudship's mythic character: a vessel that knows the strait-paths between places that aren't quite places. See `cosmology.md`, *On cloudships*.

### Other Continents (named)

- **Sortalde**: Basque *sortaldea* "sunrise-land / east" → shortened and softened to **Sortalde**. The **Talanese name** for the Eastern continent: a petal-shaped archipelago **on the far side of the Cloud Sea**, east of Talan, reachable **only by cloudship** (ordinary hulls cannot cross the vapor). **Sortalde itself builds no cloudships**: the petals see no point in the craft and hold that the outside world is at loss for being outside, and every vessel on the Talan-Sortalde route is Talan-made and Talan-operated, including the cloudships that ferry Sortalde diplomats to the Emerald Isles embassy. Surviving Talanese merchants usually only trade on its outer petals; the inner courts are harder to reach. Talanese atlases, Iron Tide intelligence, Adventurers' Guild dispatches, and Lautaran merchant records all use this name.
- **Tao Hua Yuan** (桃花源): the **Sortalde-internal name** for the same continent. Chinese-flavoured internal register (deliberately distinct from the kitsune's Japanese-flavoured Emarrean register; kitsune are *not* related to Sortalde, the parallel is coincidental). Literally "Peach Blossom Spring," after the foundational Tao Yuanming poem about a hidden paradise of peach orchards reachable only through a difficult river passage and lost to outside contact. The name encodes the continent's cultural self-understanding: *we are blossoms, we are hidden, the way back is rarely found.* Used by Sortalde residents internally and by Talanese characters who've actually been there. **Distinguishing pattern:** same external/internal split that Emarrea/Biozuri and Fenurra/Aeris already use; Talanese-stratum old-Basque externally, internal register held by the resident culture itself.

### Sortalde proper nouns (Chinese-flavoured internal register)

The seven petals of Sortalde each carry a Sortalde-internal name. Pattern: a one- or two-character descriptor + *Dao* (岛, "island") for the six ancestral petals; the central Concord seat uses *Heting* (合廷, "Concord-Court"). Chinese-flavoured throughout, with no claim to literal Mandarin grammar; the names are evocative compounds, not phrasebook entries.

- **Lundao** (轮岛, "Wheel Island"): Samsaran homeland. Named for *samsara* (the wheel of reincarnation). The senior contemplative-bureaucratic petal; Samsaran chancellors keep multi-life memory of the Concord's history.
- **Niudao** (牛岛, "Ox Island"): Sarangay homeland. Named directly for the bull-folk's bovine character. The warrior-oath petal; Sarangay houses serve multiple courts as standing martial nobility.
- **Xidao** (戏岛, "Theatre Island"): Tanuki homeland. Named for *xi* (play, theatre, deception). The convivial-merchant-performer petal; Tanuki troupes circulate Sortalde and occasionally reach Talan via surviving merchant routes.
- **Yingdao** (影岛, "Shadow Island"): Wayang homeland. Named for *ying* (shadow). The shadow-court petal; Wayang serve as inter-petal spies, diplomats, and shadow-screen archivists; most secrets in Sortalde have passed through Yingdao at some point.
- **Lingdao** (灵岛, "Spirit Island"): Yaksha homeland, also the **concentration zone for bound Oni** (see *Oni* below). Named for *ling* (spirit). The dynastic-place-spirit petal; Yaksha are nobility tied to specific lands here, and Oni are their darker cousins; shared spirit-nature, opposed temperament.
- **Wandao** (万岛, "Myriad Island"): Yaoguai homeland. Named for *wan* (myriad, ten thousand). The common-folk petal; Yaoguai's vast taxonomic spectrum (beast-shaped sapients of countless variations) makes Wandao the most populous petal and the one most easily reached by Talanese merchants.
- **Heting** (合廷, "Concord-Court"): the central seat. A smaller island at the geographic centre of the seven-petal arrangement. The **Concord of Courts** convenes here; ambassadors from each of the six ancestral petals maintain residence. Reaching Heting from Talan is mythically hard; most Talanese never hear it named. The Emarrean kitsune (entirely unrelated to Sortalde) have a coincidentally similar internal register, which means the two cultures' diplomats can sometimes pronounce each other's names but cannot trust each other's grammar.

### Sortalde institutions

- **The Concord of Courts**: the long-standing confederation of the six ancestral petals, convening at Heting. Held together by ancient agreement rather than enforced authority. Samsaran chancellors are the continuity (multi-life memory carries the Concord's institutional knowledge across human lifetimes).
- **Oni**: bound spirits of Sortalde tied to specific places. Concentrated on **Lingdao** alongside the Yaksha (who are their *lighter* cousins; both spirit-natures, opposed temperaments). Manifest physically only at their bound site; cannot wander. **Half-Oni mortal children are Hungerseeds** (see `ancestries.md` heritages).
- **Tao Hua Yuan ships**: Sortalde-built indigenous craft adapted across generations to the rough and strange character of Sortalde's *inner sea* (the waters between the petals where the inner-petal courts and the central Concord seat at Heting are sited). Name borrows the continent's own internal name (桃花源, *Tao Hua Yuan*, "Peach Blossom Spring"), so the craft are named "ships of the peach-blossom land." Talan-built cloudships cannot weather the inner-sea conditions and land only at the outer petals (Wandao, Xidao, Niudao); from there, onward travel into Sortalde's interior requires a Tao Hua Yuan ship. Boarding is a Concord question and is one of the petals' quieter gatekeeping mechanisms; Talanese passengers very rarely board them. **The structural reason Talanese merchants reach only the outer petals**, and the practical reason the inner courts and Heting remain rumour to most Talanese. The inner sea itself is unnamed in canon; whether it has a Sortalde-internal name is canon-pending.

### Named Non-Bound Beings (Layer 1 Primordials)

The nine Layer-1-tier primordials: six plane-bound to the elemental and energy strata (Layer 1), Iturima holding a unique position in the Wellspring (Layer 1), and **two Layer-2 wanderers** (Zurzar in Feyworld for Wood, Burdinzar in Shadowplane for Metal) who occasionally cross into the Material Plane. The six plane-bound share the *-zar* suffix marking the deep-ancient stratum (cf. Zaharsuge, the wider *-zar* family); Iturima follows the *-arima* "soul" pattern instead (cf. Epairima); the two Layer-2 residents also use the *-zar* suffix, marking them in the same age-tier as the plane-bound six though they sit on a different plane-layer. The two Layer-2 residents are bound by the Compact in proportion to power while on the Life Layer (weakly on Feyworld and Shadowplane, fully on the Material Plane); they work through clerics, omens, and undirected substrate-pressure rather than direct action. **Each primordial grants its specific elemental or substrate cleric domain** (Suzar→Fire, Urzar→Water, Lurzahar→Earth, Haizar→Air, Indazar→Healing, Hutzar→Nothingness, Iturima→Soul, Zurzar→Wood, Burdinzar→Metal), all cross-pantheon Non-Bound co-grants; the primordials do not judge, imposing no moral anathema beyond what the element itself imposes. Full canon: `gods.md`, *Named Non-Bound Gods → Layer-1 Primordials*.

- **Suzar**: Basque *su* (fire) + *zahar* (ancient) → *suzar*. Major Being of the fire-stratum of the Elemental Plane (Layer 1). Warms the cold, consumes what stops moving; indifferent to which.
- **Urzar**: Basque *ur* (water) + *zahar* → *urzar*. Major Being of the water-stratum (Layer 1). Soothing as the rain, unforgiving as the flood; either gives life or takes it.
- **Lurzahar**: Basque *lur* (earth) + *zahar* → *lurzahar*. Major Being of the earth-and-stone stratum (Layer 1). Longer form deliberately retained to distinguish from the Brauogi place-name *Lurrath*. Slow, immovable, generative; holds everything up and eventually swallows everything.
- **Haizar**: Basque *haize* (wind) + *zahar* → *haizar*. Major Being of the wind-and-air stratum (Layer 1). Restless, fickle; brings the storm and the gentle breath without preference.
- **Indazar**: Basque *indar* (force, strength, vigour) + *zahar* → *indazar*. Major Being of the positive/life current of the Energy Plane (Layer 1). Generative, exuberant, indiscriminate; pushes things to grow, with no distinction between a child and a cancer.
- **Hutzar**: Basque *huts* (void, empty) + *zahar* → *hutzar*. Major Being of the negative/void current of the Energy Plane (Layer 1). Quiet, patient; the hollow that waits.
- **Iturima**: Basque *iturri* (spring, source) + *arima* (soul) → *iturarima → iturima*. Major Being (canon-pending tier), the soul of the Wellspring itself, the most fundamental of the nine Layer-1 primordials. Not gendered, not characterised, possibly not sentient in any sense mortals would recognise as personhood; worshipped almost nowhere.
- **Zurzar**: Basque *zur* (wood) + *zahar* (ancient) → *zurzar*. Major Being of the wood-substance. **Layer-2 wandering**: resides primarily in **Feyworld** (where wood-element rules), with occasional Material-Plane visits to deep forests, Brauogi mineral-Magitech workshops, and wild-growth sites. Compact-bound in proportion to power on the Life Layer (weakly on Feyworld, fully on the Material Plane); works through clerics and omens, not direct action. Patient as the tree, supple as the sapling, dry as the dead branch.
- **Burdinzar**: Basque *burdina* (iron, metal) + *zahar* (ancient) → *burdinazar* → drift to **Burdinzar** (loss of unstressed *-a-*). Major Being of the metal-substance, ore and edge. **Layer-2 wandering**: resides primarily in **Shadowplane** (where metal-element rules), with occasional Material-Plane visits to mines, forges, and deep ruins where ancient steel sleeps. Compact-bound in proportion to power on the Life Layer (weakly on Shadowplane, fully on the Material Plane); works through clerics and omens, not direct action. Patient as the seam in stone, sharp as the worked blade, lasting as the buried thing.

### Named Non-Bound Gods (Layer 3 residents)

Full canon in `gods.md`, *Named Non-Bound Gods*.

- **Epairima**: Basque *epai* (judgment, sentence) + *arima* (soul) → *epai-arima* → assimilated to **Epairima** "soul-judgment." Minor God; **Judge of Souls** of Dauria, the City of the Dead. Resides in **Diyu** (Layer 3, neutral). Each soul that reaches Dauria unclaimed is weighed at her court; her verdict routes the soul onward to its god, onward to the Wellspring, or (rarely) back to the layer above for a second pass.
- **Bikiargi**: Basque *biki* (twin) + *argi* (light) → *bikiargi* "the twin-light." Major God of the **Moon**, in two persons sharing one portfolio. The twin-halves correspond to the two named moons: **Unaru** is the Bright Twin (benevolent: calm tides, safe roads, true dreams); **Veyru** is the Dark Twin (malevolent: ill omens, hunting weather for what shouldn't be hunted, false dreams). Together they are neutral, which is why they reside in **Diyu** (Layer 3, neutral). Two women at apparent age twenty-three, the *younger siblings* of Solyra in folk-faith iconography. Worshipped by sailors, navigators, witches, lovers, astronomers; anyone whose work depends on moonlight or tide. **Lautaran trade-fleets** and **Floteyn island-pilots** are the named seafaring traditions that keep separate shrines to each twin. Full standalone entries for Unaru and Veyru follow below.
- **Veyru**: Old Norse *vei* (woe, alas; the poetic interjection of lament) + *rún* (rune, mystery, secret) → ***Veirún*** "the woe-rune," the omen of woe. Drifted to **Veyru** through loss of final *n* in unstressed position and vowel-shift in the diphthong *ei* → *ey* under lunar invocation cadence. The **Dark Twin** of Bikiargi (red moon, malevolent face): ill omens, hunting weather for what shouldn't be hunted, false dreams, sleepwalkers led to cliffs, the moonlit hunt that becomes the hunted. Pairs by suffix-drift with Unaru (the Bright Twin); both names share the *-rún → -ru* drift pattern, marking them as opposite-pole twins. Mortals who try to invoke only Veyru inevitably draw Unaru too; the Twins do not separate. Full canon: `gods.md`, *Bikiargi: The Twins*.
- **Unaru**: Old Norse *unað* (delight, joy, contentment, bliss) + *rún* (rune, mystery) → ***Unaðrún*** "the delight-rune," the rune of contentment. Drifted to **Unaru** through loss of medial *ð*, loss of final *n* in unstressed position, and accent loss. The **Bright Twin** of Bikiargi (green moon, benevolent face): calm tides, safe roads under moonlight, true dreams, lovers reunited, the moonlit hunt that comes home well-fed. Pairs by suffix-drift with Veyru (the Dark Twin); both names share the *-rún → -ru* drift pattern. Mortals who try to invoke only Unaru inevitably draw Veyru too. Full canon: `gods.md`, *Bikiargi: The Twins*.
- **Zaharsuge**: Basque *suge* (serpent) + *zahar* (ancient) → *zahar-suge* → assimilated to **Zaharsuge** "the ancient one of serpents." Minor God of **Wyrmkin** (drakes, basilisks, primal serpents, indigenous Talan dragon-line). Predates the alien Dragons' arrival by ages; their line of indigenous wyrms was already established when the Dragon mothership crashed. **Pronouns: they / them / their** (neither male nor female; the wyrm-line predates the gendered ancestries mortals know; full note in `gods.md`). Resides in **Diyu** (Layer 3, neutral). Pairs with Ehizahar (Hinka's domain) and **Hilezar** (rejected naming proposal, *not* used) as the *-zar* suffix family marking the deep-ancient stratum across multiple proper nouns. **GM-tier secret:** Zaharsuge is **belief-formed**, not primordial. The Grand Gods made the original Wyrmkin civilisation; the Elden enslaved and diminished them into Dragonets during the Elden Era; Dragonets attributed their creation to *Zaharsuge*, and the god coalesced from that belief sometime during the late Gods' Era or Lost Era leak window. Full canon in `timeline.md`, *Elden Era → ⚿ GM Secret: The Wyrmkin: The Real History*.
- **Betibizi**: Basque *betiko* (eternal/forever) + *bizi* (life, alive) → *betiko-bizi* "eternal life" → assimilated to **Betibizi**. Minor God of **Undeath**. Resides in **Abyss** (Layer 3, evil). **Self-named** at the moment of ascension; the name is a declaration, not a gift. **Origin:** a Storveldi Denbora ruling-class survivor who ascended via the **integration procedure** in the aftermath of the Week of Crimson Rain; he is the *original* holder of that procedure and the reason it persists at all. The Blackened Lands' natural-born Skeleton ancestry and ambient necromantic resonance trace spiritually back to him, though the proximate cause is the cursed ground itself. Major thread: the integration procedure (see `timeline.md`, *Gods' Era → ⚿ GM Secret: The Storveldi Denbora: The Real History*) now has a canonical owner.
- **Solyra**: Icelandic *sól* (sun; also the personified sun in Old Norse and Icelandic mythic vocabulary) + *ára* (genitive plural of *ár* "year," meaning "of the ages") → *Sólára* "the sun of the ages." Drifted to **Solyra** across millennia of mortal usage; long-mark loss on *á* and unstressed-vowel reduction in the middle syllable. **Major God of the Sun.** Resides in **Diyu** (Layer 3, neutral). The **elder sibling** of the celestial trio: her prayer-current reached deity-tier first, since sun-awe predates lunar mysticism by long ages. A single being, always depicted alone (never paired), woman of apparent age thirty, **clothed only in her own light** (unhidden but unseeable, like the sun itself). Distinct from Iro's *Light* portfolio: Iro's Light is the theological power (dawn, hope, growth, zealotry, Compact-bound); Solyra is the sun-disc itself (distant, sancta-less, the older awe). Folk-faith keeps the line cleanly. Full canon in `gods.md`, *Solyra: The Sun of the Ages*.
- **Odain**: Icelandic *ódáinn* (the deathless, the undying) → drifted to **Odain** across four millennia of Dragon-internal religious usage. **Minor God, belief-formed, GM-tier secret.** Pulled into existence by four millennia of purist Dragon conviction at Dragon's Reach: the platonic remembered-image of *what Dragons were before the corruption* given a face. Resides in **Diyu** (Layer 3, neutral). Sancta-less, clerical-lineage-less, no public worship. Odain blesses sustained purist discipline with a structural extension of lifespan against the Wellspring cycle (the only kind of blessing the god's shape can give), which is the actual mechanism behind the Disciplined-purist longevity differential. *No living Dragon recognises Odain as a god*: purists experience the blessing as their own discipline working, pragmatists experience its absence as the doctrine failing. Cannot deliver the gift the doctrine actually promises (true immortality is a substrate problem; see Dragon corruption secret). Full canon in `gods.md`, *Odain*.

### Pre-Crimson-Rain civilisations & their fall

- **The Wyrmkin**: Plain English. Modern Talanese folk-name for the now-extinct sapient indigenous serpentine civilisation of Talan, ancestral to the modern Dragonet ancestry. Built a city in the deep substrate during the early Gods' Era; were conquered and enslaved by the Elden during the Elden Era (6000–2945 GR); the diminished form their enslaved descendants now wear is the Dragonet. Their original self-name was lost in the conquest. The modern term *the Wyrmkin* doubles as the generic for Zaharsuge's serpentine portfolio, the dual usage being a linguistic legacy of the people's fall (the name of the people became the name of what they had been after the people were gone). Full canon in `timeline.md`, *Elden Era → ⚿ GM Secret: The Wyrmkin: The Real History*; deeply connected to the GM-tier truth about **Zaharsuge** (belief-formed by Dragonet faith, not actually the wyrms' creator).
- **The Storveldi Denbora**: Icelandic *stór* "great" + *veldi* "empire/dominion" + Basque *denbora* "time/era" = literally "the era of the great empire." Mixed-language deep-old compound, fitting the Gods'-Era stratum. A mortal civilisation of the Gods' Era (2944 GR – 1 GR) that settled atop Elden ruins, **falsely claimed Elden descent for prestige**, and ran a generations-long programme of divine self-engineering. Their true edge was developing the **integration procedure**: the technique for imbuing a found shard of divinity into a mortal's own divine essence. Most of their ruling class self-elevated to Demi-God status by belief-and-power accumulation; two reached Minor God status by integrating recovered shards. **The procedure is not public knowledge, but it persists** somewhere on Talan or its veils. **They killed Tani.** Their homeland was annihilated in the Week of Crimson Rain's divine retaliation. The remnants of their experimented-upon population persist today as the **Azarketi** ancestry (most of whom still inherit the original Elden-blood lie). Full canon in `timeline.md`, *Gods' Era → ⚿ GM Secret: The Storveldi Denbora: The Real History*.
- **Shard of divinity**: fragment of divine power that can be **found**, not granted. No god's consent needed to use one. Sources: ancient ruins, godblood battlefields, killing another Minor or Major God (near-impossible), the Aurora Veil and Duskmire veil, and other planes where gods have died. Use requires the **integration procedure** (see Storveldi Denbora). Detailed in `cosmology.md`, *The Mortal Ascent Ladder*.
- **The God Killing Incident**: Plain English. The mortal folk-name for the killing of Tani. The phrase strips the killers from the story; most modern usage doesn't know who they were.
- **The Blackened Lands / The Black Spot on the Map**: Plain English. The cursed-ground scar at the southern coast of Lioaru where Tani was struck down and the Storveldi Denbora capital was annihilated. Canonically the same place as the **Lost Kingdom** sub-region (see `geography/lioaru.md`). Origin of the **Fleshwarp** and **Skeleton** ancestries.
- **Lost Kingdom**: Plain English. Modern-map name for the Blackened Lands sub-region of Lioaru. Already entered above under Lioaru sub-regions; cross-listed here for the GM-tier-archaeology reader.

### The Compact of the Bound Thirteen (post-Week of Crimson Rain cosmology)

Full text and structure in `cosmology.md`, *The Gods' Law: The Compact of the Bound Thirteen*.

- **The Gods' Law.** Modern English, mortal-mouth name. The most common name for the body of binding sworn at the close of the Week of Crimson Rain.
- **The Compact.** Modern English, scholarly register. The formal name; used by chroniclers and clergy when writing about the binding as a document.
- **The Thirteen Articles.** Modern English. Chronicler designation when the binding is enumerated article-by-article rather than treated as a single body of law.
- **Sanctioned Exchanges.** Modern English. The catalog of canonical exchange-forms permitted under Articles 2 through 5. Four named forms in current canon: **The Clergy Bond** (lifetime worship → continuous grants), **The Knight's Oath** (lifetime service in arms → martial grants; the paladin form), **The Common Blessing** (defined rite + offering → defined favour), **The Lifetime Covenant** (lifetime devotion of a specific kind → bespoke grant; Araphel's second-chance bond is the model). The catalog is open.
- **Anathema.** Modern English, ecclesiastical. The named acts whose commission by a cleric breaks the covenant of Article 5 and frees their god from the binding. Every church codifies its own list so the line is legible in advance.
- **The binding within the binding.** Chronicler phrase for Article 13, the recursive article that sworn each signatory to enforce the Compact against any other signatory who would violate it.

#### Voices on the Compact

- **Golivander.** The most-travelled chronicler of Talan; a known name in the literate classes of every continent kingdom that prints books. Full name **Golivander Tessek**; he signs *Golivander* in print and *G.* in column sign-offs, with *Tessek* appearing only at formal introduction.
  - **Race and birth.** **Tengu**, born on **Haizetsua** (the great western tengu island of Vindul). His Haizetsua volume in the *Traveller's Guide* is the standing reference on Tengu life for the rest of Talan, written from a child's-deep insider familiarity; he goes home rarely now but writes the island with the authority no other Talan-side scholar can match. His Tengu lifespan (150–200) is what makes the multi-decade chronicler career structurally possible.
  - **Age.** Approximately one hundred and ten in 2532 MR. Slate-and-charcoal feathering with the white-brow signature of a mature Tengu, spectacled, ink-stained finger-claws, a worn travelling satchel that goes with him everywhere. Dresses well enough for court and no more lavishly than that.
  - **Disposition.** Loves to travel and to experience the world; intensely curious; tries everything once and writes about it whether the trying went well or poorly. The catjomin tasting at Kawaakari, the Foxfire Stone Massage, the third trial of the Onyx Spectacle from a row-C box-stand seat: all the same impulse. He travels alone, three-quarters of the year on the road.
  - **Education.** Apprenticed at **Thekkavar** (the City of Learning, Ezkudon) some seventy years ago in natural philosophy and rhetoric. Apprentice-and-leave path, not the degree-and-stay one. He cites **Jeanne Pintos** as the model from his Thekkavar reading; her *You're not flying, you're falling upwards* is the treatise he treats as the working text for natural philosophy and the basis for treating the Gods' Law as physics-not-rule.
  - **Magical training.** Modest **Occult**, picked up alongside the natural-philosophy work at Thekkavar; not a practising caster of consequence. Enough to read certain Wellspring-touched inscriptions when the column needs it. The fit is thematic as much as practical: the Chronicle's life-work *is* cultural-memory residue (see [cosmology.md](cosmology.md), *Magic: The Four Schools → Occult*), and Golivander adds to that residue every time a Heart quotes him at tea.
  - **Home base.** A small permanent room above **The Lantern Press** in **Frae City**, his only fixed address. He is rarely there; the room holds his back-files, his correspondence, and the icon of Iro the publisher's father left over the desk and that Halvar has never moved.
  - **Publisher.** **The Lantern Press** of Frae City, run by **Halvar Erikson** (human, ~60s, second-generation; his father founded the press in the post-Crimson-Year print boom). Halvar's daughter **Maren Erikson** is learning the trade and is the one who reads Golivander's drafts before they go to set. The financial arrangement is per-column and per-volume; the *Traveller's Guide* reprints are the steady income; the *Chronicle* subscription list (Lautara, Frae City, Sumendar, smaller in Sortalde) funds his travel.
  - **Scholarly stance.** Naturalistic and even-handed. Treats the Compact as physics, not as moral covenant; writes about non-bound traditions (the Kyūbi-no-Den, the Faith's god, the Vice Demons-by-rumour-only) with the same unmystified respect he extends to the bound thirteen. **Not bound to any church.** Drops incense at any temple he passes on the road as travel-habit, not devotion. The Forseti clergies of Lograth are slightly cool on him because the physics-not-rule framing reads as morally evasive in their tradition; the Cronus and Araphel clergies welcome him; the Adventurers' Guild educators freely cite him.
  - **Method.** Three working rules, established over the career:
    1. **Never print uncorroborated gossip.** What the kingdom is saying may appear in the column; what the kingdom is *whispering* may not, unless he can corroborate it independently.
    2. **Protect sources by deliberate elision.** The signature *I shall not name* and *the Heart who keeps that quiet knows already* patterns in *Letters from Biozuri* are not coyness; they are method.
    3. **Revise when corrected in print.** Saemi (Azure Heart of Emarrea) once corrected him on a misattribution and suggested he revise the column before it went to press; he did, and the practice has become a standing offer to every subject. **Refuses payment from subjects**: gifts of food, lodging, and Catjomin are accepted; coin is not.
  - **Writing voice.** Experience-first, lightly witty, objective. The wit is the seasoning, never the dish. He writes what he tried, what he saw, what he was told; he reports the kingdom's argument over an event without rendering verdict; he marks the boundary between *what is the case* and *what is being said about what is the case* and never erases it. Formal-philosophical register in the books; arch-knowing-chronicler register in the periodical.
  - **Recurring relationships in canon (small at present; will grow).** **Halvar Erikson** of The Lantern Press (publisher, Frae City). In Emarrea: **Saemi** (Azure Heart; correction-and-revise), **Yorimichi** (retired Crimson; Catjomin host at Kawaakari), **Shirae** (Sapphire Heart and head of the House of Names; admitted to the archive on terms; leaves offering at the lantern before each consultation). Other named figures across Talan will land as future stories surface them.
- ***A Traveller's Guide to Tyrnarra***. Golivander's formal multi-volume reference work. The official product: formal-philosophical voice, treated by literate-class Talanese as the standing reference on everything from continental geography to the Gods' Law. Named volumes include **The Gods' Law** (the most-quoted; the epigraph on [gods-law.html](../gods-law.html) is drawn from it, and chronicler glosses cite him by volume) and **Haizetsua** (the standing reference on Tengu life, written from his child's-deep familiarity with the island). Reprinted often; available wherever books are sold across Talan.
- ***The Travelling Chronicle***. Golivander's periodical, distinct in register from his books. Regional dispatches written wherever he is at the time. Circulates across Lautara, Frae City, Sumendar, and a smaller subscription in Sortalde. The Emarrea-specific section is *Letters from Biozuri*; supplements append regional events (e.g. *Kawaakari supplement* for Yorimichi's Catjomin tastings). Tone is arch-knowing-chronicler, deeply familiar with the people he writes about, folk-belief-tier reportage (he never confirms what cannot be confirmed). Plain English title; pre-rule naming convention.
- ***Letters from Biozuri***. The Emarrea-specific section of *The Travelling Chronicle*. Sixteen years of running coverage of the Heartcourt and its surrounding kingdom-life. The kitsune themselves read his columns; some Hearts cultivate him, some pointedly do not. Full text of the present canonical dispatches lives in [`heartcourt-letters.md`](heartcourt-letters.md).
- **Jeanne Pintos.** Talanese natural philosopher; author of *You're not flying, you're falling upwards*, the in-world treatise on the laws of physics and magic that Golivander cites as the model for treating the Gods' Law as physics-not-rule.

### Geography & Settlements
- **Midarra**: Icelandic *mið* (middle, midway) + *ár* (river, water) + *-a* (definite article, reanalyzed onto the compound) → *Miðára > Midarra* "the Middle Waters." The central freshwater inner sea of Talan, NOT a city. Cronus's island (Freedom domain) sits within it; Millhaven is on its southern shore. The name predates mortal settlement; a deep-old Icelandic-drift designation for the sea that lies in the middle of the continent.
- **Hafra**: the saltwater sea surrounding Talan. Icelandic *haf* (ocean, open sea) → shortened and drifted.
- **Twin Cities**: modern English; the mobile pirate capital adrift in Midarra. A paired settlement: a water-bound raft-city of lashed hulls below, a sky-bound sister-city of tethered airships and lifted islets above. The pair moves together on a course set by the council of Pirate Lords. Sovereign: no god, no kingdom.

### Wellspring sites

- **Stillpool**: modern English (descriptive folk term, no drift). A residual Wellspring-touched site left over from the **Lost-Era leak**, when the bound thirteen alone were too few to mediate the Wellspring's outflow after the Crimson Rain. Scattered across Talan; **the count is deliberately uncatalogued in canon**: sources disagree, and even the Adventurers Guild's sealed atlas is presumed incomplete. Quiet, locally folkloric: fairy-haunted, witch-touched, bad-luck-to-visit. **Mechanic:** when a mortal interacts with a Stillpool (looks too long, bathes, drinks, leaves something behind), there is a chance that a **Reflection** is born nearby roughly one week later. Every modern Reflection traces back to a Stillpool event. The **Adventurers Guild keeps the sealed atlas** as a working register for high-rank parties; one or two Stillpools are watched by the nearest church; most are remote enough that nobody visits. See `cosmology.md`, *Wellspring Outflow: Mediation, Leakage, and the Lost-Era Window* and `timeline.md`, *Lost Era → ⚿ GM Secret: The Lost Era: The Real History*.
