# Brauogi: Earth · Sarrum

**Borders:** Vindul, Ehizahar, Myrkono

**Seas:** Hafra (western coast) and Midarra (eastern and south-eastern coasts; Twin Suns; Soul Tree island offshore).

**Etymology:** Icelandic *brauð* (bread) + Basque *ogi* (bread) → fused to *Brauogi*. Both roots mean bread; the breadbasket named twice over in two ancient tongues.

**Position:** Northwest-central Talan, directly south of Vindul. Neighbours: Vindul (north), Ehizahar (east), Midarra (east/southeast), Floteyn (southeast corner), Myrkono (south); Hafra coast to the west. Twin Suns and Soul Tree sit on the Midarra-touching corner.

**Terrain:** Rolling fertile lowlands cut by dense river networks; most of the domain's rivers spring from **Iturmen**, the central east-west mountain spine, with their headwaters in **Iturburu**, the great mountain-lake under Iturmen's tallest peak. **Eraztumen**, the ring of peaks around Lurrath, gives Gotorlekua its Stronghold character. **Mugamen**, the southern range, walls Brauogi off from Myrkono's deep forests. Western dark-mountains shelter the domain from weather rolling in off the Hafra coast. Agricultural heartland in earth-tones throughout: deep greens, brown river-soil, grey-and-rust mountain-stone, the gold of late-summer fields.

**Cultural character:** Earth-domain, Sarrum-the-Steadfast at its centre. Old noble houses control river trade and the granaries; the three-way ancestry politics (Goblin / Hobgoblin / Minotaur) is more cooperative than feud-prone because the rivers and roads have always been worth more to share than to fight over. The breadbasket feeds Vindul, Ehizahar, Myrkono, and Floteyn through nearby supply lines; the south-toward-Lautara grain trade runs across Midarra. The domain reads as the steady counterweight to its neighbours' more dramatic registers: Vindul's storms, Myrkono's shadow, Ehizahar's wild, Floteyn's water.

**Peoples:** Three dominant ancestries are anchored here: **Goblin**, **Hobgoblin**, and **Minotaur**. Sarrum himself is depicted as a Minotaur, and Brauogi's Minotaurs are his cultural cousins. The three-way of Earth-domain politics is more cooperative than feud-prone; they trade rather than fight.

**God's city:** Lurrath, The Steadfast City. Sits in Gotorlekua, the stronghold sub-region at the domain's interior.

**Sub-regions:**
- **Gotorlekua**: houses Lurrath, Sarrum's city-state, set within the ring of peaks called **Eraztumen** that gives the sub-region its Stronghold character. *(Basque gotor "strong, firm" + leku "place" + -a "the": "the Stronghold.")* The defensible interior heart of the domain; the city-state and the sub-region grew together inside the mountain ring.
- **The Earth Realm**: the agricultural heart of Brauogi between Iturmen (north) and Mugamen (south); the broad fertile plains of the breadbasket. Fully golden plains — grain, livestock, market-towns at every road-fork, the daily-bread of the northern domains. Hirubaso, the sacred druid grove, sits as an enclave in its south-centre but is its own independent sub-region (see below).
- **Hirubaso**: an independent sub-region in the south-centre of Brauogi, the sacred druid grove of Sarrum where three rivers converge in the otherwise open plains. *(Basque hiru "three" + baso "forest", drifted from hiru-ibai-baso "three-river-forest" via loss of medial ibai.)* Tended by an order of Sarrum's faithful, Hirubaso is the still point of the breadbasket: the wild left untouched at the heart of the cultivated world. Governed by the druids' order itself; independent of the surrounding Earth Realm. **The only routine Primotech site on the continent**, where Brauogi's legendary mineral-Magitech tradition (see `../cosmology.md`) finds its grown-not-built source; the rumoured Brauogi heart-stone is grown here.
- **Baratalda**: the eastern arm of Brauogi at the Ehizahar border. *(Basque baratze "garden, orchard" + aldea "region" → drift drops the medial z to give Baratalda.)* Mixed golden plain and green orchard country; the orchard-and-fruit-cellar of Brauogi where the harvest-fairs draw Ehizahar hunters down from the Lands of Villtur to trade game and furs for wine, apple-brandy, and stone-fruit. The frontier-trade kingdom of the bread-domain.
- **Azkamour**: border territory straddling Vindul and Brauogi, **capital on the Vindul side**. *(Basque azkar "swift" + muga "border": "the Swift March.")* Mostly golden Brauogi-character grain territory inland, but the territory includes the Vindul coastal stretch where the storm-fishing routes meet the grain-roads; the fast riders carry both ways. See also [Vindul](vindul.md).
- **Twin Suns**: coastal sub-region on the Midarra-touching corner, the Brauogi-side half of the **Twin Suns / Bikitsa** border kingdom shared with Myrkono. Golden, food-rich coast where the two-sun phenomenon is observed at certain seasons; Soul Tree island lies offshore. The Myrkono-side counterpart (**Bikitsa**, Basque *biki-itzal* "twin-shadow", see [Myrkono](myrkono.md)) sees the same phenomenon from its shadow-domain side; the two halves are jointly governed under a single structure (form canon-pending). Modern English name; origin of the "twin suns" framing canon-pending (see `../../docs/open-threads.md`).
- **Soul Tree**: a small rocky island in Midarra, just off the coast of Twin Suns. Named for the single massive pale tree at its centre, an actual tree, through which souls may wander on to the Postlife. One of the most cosmologically significant sites on Talan: a natural passage point between the Life Layer and the Postlife that does not require death in the conventional sense. **Voroir Daua oldest charter**: the cycle-warden order maintains a permanent presence directly on the island, and this charter is their *oldest* (predates the Lograth / Thousand-Kingdom / Myrkono-edge presence noted in `../factions.md`). The site's natural-soul-passage role is exactly the cosmological function the order exists to watch, so charter primacy follows. The thematic match between a great rooted tree and Sarrum-the-Steadfast's Earth-domain affiliation is the cosmological resonance behind the sub-region's Brauogi attribution. Modern English name.

**Note on Iturmen + Iturburu**: the central spine (Iturmen) and the source-lake under its tallest peak (Iturburu) are unclaimed natural territory at the heart of the domain — neither belongs to any sub-region. The pass-towns on the spine where the trade-roads cross answer to themselves; the source-lake and its surrounding peaks are tended by druids attached to Hirubaso.
