# Vindul: Wind · Fisaya

**Borders:** Brauogi, Ehizahar

**Seas:** Hafra (Fellibylur west coast; Haizetsua's coasts).

**Etymology:** Icelandic *vindur* (wind) → shortened and softened → *Vindul*

**Position:** Northwest Talan

**Terrain:** Arctic/icy north coast, mountain range (east), ancient old-growth forest, open grass steppes (south), rivers and lakes. Massive island to the west (Haizetsua).

**God's city:** Haizava, The Shifting City

**Sub-regions:**
- **Baerfrost**: the fully arctic sub-region of Vindul, the northern coast where the ice never fully retreats. Home to the largest stable Jotunborn population on Talan and, since the Dark Era, to one of the continent's most distinctive small polities: the **Hunt-League**. During the breach centuries the arctic clans survived because they could kill what came for them, and the ones who could not died. By the close of the Dark Era the survivors had condensed their politics into a two-tier ritual institution. Each clan picks its own chieftain by its own meritocratic test (most weigh proven kills; some weigh seasons-led, hard-winters-survived, or feasts-hosted; each clan's reckoning is its own affair), and once a year at midwinter the chieftains travel to a fixed cold-hall and meet in council, voting among themselves for the year's **Hammer-Lord**, the Jotunborn whose Merit over the past year stands tallest. Authority is total within the year and ends at the next midwinter without inheritance. The kills that have won the Hammer are remembered in skald-record: corrupted-spawn stragglers in the early centuries, an ice-kraken whose teeth still hang at the hall's beam, a deep-water serpent in 2487 MR. The institution was Dark-Era survival made permanent and perfect for its era; the era is now over. Corrupted-spawn stragglers have dwindled, the deep beasts are rarer each decade, and trade flows up from Skarvorn-allied coastal traders in the south. Some border clans have begun building permanent winter-halls rather than camping, and the families who hold those halls have begun to look very much like jarldoms in everything but name. The Gathering still meets and the Hammer still passes, but the question of *what Merit is for, when the dark is gone* is the loudest argument the chieftains have ever held. A meritocracy of killing is becoming, under its own feet, a meritocracy of diplomacy: a recent Hammer-Lord won on a successful trade-treaty with the **Air Monastery**'s eastern range rather than a kill, and half the chieftains read this as a betrayal of the institution while the other half read it as the institution finally working.
- **Air Monastery**: a fortified enclave in Vindul's eastern mountain range, autonomous territory, and the mother-house of **the Wyndwalken**, the order whose cartography sets the gold standard of Talanese map-making. The order's deepest roots are pre-Imperial: an astronomer enclave founded by Kashrishi observers who climbed the eastern peaks in the late Lost Era to read the night sky from the clearest altitude on the continent. The order's secular-contemplative cast was set in that founding. The Golden Empire conquered them in its seventh-century-MR expansion and gave them in return the imperial brass: precision instruments, mechanical clockwork, geometers' standard-rules, and (the gift that would shape every later century of their work) the technique of mapping what one observes against a calibrated grid. They became the Empire's high-altitude observatories and the Empire's mountain surveyors in the same generation. When the breach centuries came and the Empire fell, the order withdrew behind its mountain walls and held; the fortress thickened, the gates closed, the brass instruments folded inside, and the surrounding refugees who climbed the mountain were taken in. Eight centuries of defensive monasticism turned the astronomer-surveyors into a contemplative order with an astronomer-surveyor's discipline still at its centre, and the fire-watch atop the highest tower has been lit every night since the breach centuries as both a navigation point for travellers and a signal that civilization persists here. When the Adventurer Era opened the world back up, the Wyndwalken left their gates. They are a mendicant order now: most monks spend most of their working lives on the road, walking the ranges and coasts and forests of Talan with rule, compass, and field-book, returning to the mother-house only for retreat, archival, training, and the long winter when the eastern passes close. Their maps are the standard reference at every court that can afford a copy; the most prized are said to show *the way a place wants to be found*, an Occult-touched cartography that has never been satisfactorily explained outside the order. The Wyndwalken take their vows on observation and craft rather than on a god's name, and the Kashrishi philosophical cast has held since the founding: theology kept private, civic work made public, the order itself the public-civic crown of that inclination. Most Wyndwalken are Kashrishi, and the Air Monastery is the closest thing the Kashrishi ancestry has to a home-institution on Talan; the remainder are mostly other-Vindul (Tengu, Human), with a steady minority of aspirants from outside the domain drawn by the order's reputation.
- **Fellibylur**: the storm-wracked sub-region between Baerfrost in the north (separated by the river that runs east through the lands of Villtur), Azkamour to the southwest, and Brauogi to the south and east, with the **Hafra coast** to the west. Open grass-steppe with scattered foothills and one notable interior forest, river-cut, storm-weather year-round. The name *Fellibylur* (Icelandic *fell* "lone mountain" + *bylur* "blizzard") originally described a literal storm-peak in the deep-old usage; the peak-reading faded over centuries and the name persisted as a description of the *region's character*, where storms gather and roll across open lands. Strix are the kingdom's anchor ancestry, common across both halves and dominant in the interior; the coast mixes them with Tengu sailors and Kashrishi-Human merchant-families. The kingdom is governed under **the Stormpact**: a chartered Council of two seats. **The Skybell Republic** holds the coast and the Hafra ports, named for the consecrated bronze storm-warning bells rung from each port-tower when storms are inbound; it registers chartered merchant-houses, certifies **Stormriders** (the kingdom's signature export, foul-weather Hafra pilots hired across Talan for cloud-sea crossings nothing born outside Fellibylur can read fast enough), and maintains the bell-network. **The Thunderhost** holds the steppe-interior and the clan-circuits, a moot of Strix clan-chieftains who meet at midsummer at a fixed inland gathering to deliberate matters of grazing, horse-breeding, weather-lore, and the joint Stormpact business. The two seats vote together on questions that touch both halves of the kingdom (trade-charters with Baerfrost's Hunt-League, Wyndwalken cartographer commissions for the coast, disputes that cross the coast-interior line) and rule their own halves between sessions. The Stormpact itself was sworn in the early Adventurer Era when the post-Dark-Era trade re-opening brought Baerfrost caravans south and southern merchants north through the same Fellibylur passes for the first time in centuries; the two halves of the region had to agree on a shared law or be ruined by competing for the same traffic, and the Stormpact is the agreement that held. Fisaya is the kingdom's unifying faith: storms are read as the goddess speaking, and reading her is both the Skybell Republic's commercial practice (the bell-network is Fisaya-consecrated infrastructure) and the Thunderhost's spiritual practice (the chieftains take their seats on the strength of demonstrated storm-augury). The most prestigious Stormriders train for years at both sides: a Wyndwalken-quality field-book in one hand and a Thunderhost-trained ear for thunder in the other.
- **Haizetsua**: the great western island of Vindul, Fisaya's domain, home of the Tengu. Mountain-range interior, coastal lowlands to the south and east, a sheltered harbour at the capital **Vindboorg**. Governed by the **Skarvorn Republic**: the seated body of the eleven Tengu clans, no single ruler. Its cities are the famous **singing cities** of the continent, dwellings carved with channels and tuned cavities so that wind passing through produces sustained musical tones. The island's signature export is the **Three Teas** (Skardun · Vintek · Korrek, in descending luxury); the **Vornsketta**, the Tengu migration custom, sends a steady stream of young Haizetsuan travellers across the continent every year. Three Pines (Myrkono's outer-island cluster) sits off Haizetsua's southern coast. (*haize* = wind in Basque; the name echoes the domain.) See [`../cultures.md`](../cultures.md), *The Tengu*, for the people, the Republic, the eleven clans, the eight winds, and the singing-city tradition.
- **Azkamour**: border territory straddling Vindul and Brauogi, **capital on the Vindul side**. *(Basque azkar "swift" + muga "border": "the Swift March.")* Mostly golden Brauogi-character grain territory inland, but the Vindul coastal stretch anchors the capital where the storm-fishing routes meet the grain-roads; the fast riders carry both ways. See also [Brauogi](brauogi.md).
