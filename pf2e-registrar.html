Cleric Domains
Every canonical PF2e domain and its Tyrnarra source

Alphabetical master register. Granted by names the in-world being whose authority backs the domain grant. Source category identifies the class of granter: for filtering at a glance. A cleric channelling a domain answers theologically to the granter named here.

Reading the categories. Bound 13 are the Compact-bound gods walking the Material Plane. Vice Demon are the seven seat-holders of Bolverk. Non-Bound covers both Layer-3 unbound deities (Solyra, Bikiargi, Zaharsuge, Epairima, Betibizi) and the nine Layer-1-tier primordials (Suzar, Urzar, Lurzahar, Haizar, Indazar, Hutzar, Iturima, and the two Layer-2 residents Zurzar and Burdinzar). Each primordial grants its specific elemental or substrate domain to faithful who reach the substance directly; the primordials do not judge — they impose no moral filter on the grant, only the element's own nature. General are the surviving Generals of Corruption, imprisoned beneath Talan but reachable by their cultists. Corrupted God covers direct grants from the imprisoned god through the Binding's leaks. Open is held in reserve for any future canon-pending domain.

No-sharing within a pantheon. Each pantheon (the Bound 13, the seven Vice Demons of Bolverk, the fourteen Virtue Devils to come) handles cleric domains under the same rule: within the pantheon, every domain is granted by exactly one member (the one whose aspects fit it most strongly within that pantheon). Cross-pantheon sharing is allowed: a domain can be granted by one Bound god AND one Vice Demon AND one Virtue Devil simultaneously, where each is the strongest fit within their own pantheon. The Granted by column lists all current grantors; a cleric reading the registrar sees every theological framing available for the domain they're channelling.

PF2e Common · Uncommon · Rare, set by where each people lives on Talan

Reading the rarities. Common No GM permission needed; the people exists across Talan or dominates a domain. Uncommon Requires GM nod; the people exists in canon but in lower numbers or specific contexts. Rare GM-restricted; a story-tier presence on Talan rather than a demographic.
