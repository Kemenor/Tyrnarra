Guild Contract · Dungeon Delve · Millhaven Branch #1,847
Below the Quiet Docks
The cargo went down. The crew went looking. Nobody came back up.
Party Level 5 3–5 Sessions Beneath Millhaven — Cavern System Dungeon Crawl + Discovery Contract: Sable Rei
The Contract

The contract appears on the quest board one morning, posted by hand — no guild intermediary, no formal submission process. Mira Seld received it directly from a Kitsune in a very good coat who set it on her desk, smiled with too many teeth showing, and said "the usual terms" as if there were usual terms. There are now.

The pay is exceptional. The listed objective is simple: locate a shipment of unspecified cargo that was offloaded via the Quiet Docks three nights ago and has not been collected. The retrieval team Sable Rei sent to find it has also not returned. The contract requests "recovery of assets and personnel, in that order of priority." The last line is crossed out and rewritten: "personnel first. assets if convenient."

Read Aloud — Quest Board
"Missing: one consignment of goods, offloaded via private dock, bridge district, three nights past. Also missing: four-person retrieval team sent to locate same. Contract offers 800gp upon recovery of personnel, 400gp additional upon recovery of cargo. Enquire with Mira Seld for access details. Discretion absolute. — S.R."
GM Note — What Sable Rei Actually Knows
The cargo was a crate of documents — correspondence between a Rika Tikur merchant house and a member of the Legea Empire's trade council, the kind of letters that are worth significant money to the right buyer and significant danger to the wrong one. Sable Rei doesn't know what's down there. They know the retrieval team is good and they haven't checked in for two nights. The fox is genuinely worried, which they express by posting an extremely professional contract and not sleeping. If the party talks to them before going in, Sable Rei will provide the access route to the Quiet Docks and a detailed description of the retrieval team. They will not mention what the cargo contains. They will answer "sensitive documents" if pressed and change the subject.
GM Note — Aldric's Position
Aldric will approve this contract with the expression of a man biting into something he expected to be sweet. He will remind the party that the guild takes no position on the legality of the cargo and will not be liable for what they find down there. He will also, quietly, tell them to be careful — not because of Sable Rei, but because four experienced people went into a hole under his town and haven't come out. That bothers him more than the politics.
What Is Actually Down There

The cavern system beneath the Millwater River's eastern bank has existed for centuries — old enough to predate Millhaven, old enough that parts of it show stonework from an era nobody has cleanly identified. Smugglers found it approximately two years ago via a drainage collapse near the Quiet Docks and have been using its upper chambers as a staging area ever since. The operation was modest, functional, and entirely unaware that the deeper caverns were already occupied.

The Venom Queen — the party's name for her is as good as any, she has no name she offers — is a Giant Spider of unusual age and unusual awareness. She did not choose the upper caverns as her territory because they were useful; she chose them because the bioluminescent spore colonies on the ceiling are her eggs, her larder, and her early warning system. The smugglers moved into her antechamber. She noticed immediately. She waited.

Three nights ago, a cargo drop disturbed a web sensor in the upper warehouse. The Queen sent three of her brood. The smugglers panicked, ran deeper into the cavern trying to find another exit, and found only more of her territory. She has been feeding since. The retrieval team arrived the following night, descended the Quiet Dock access, and has not been seen since.

The Queen's Intelligence
She is not sapient. She does not speak, plan in the abstract, or have goals beyond territory, feeding, and the protection of her eggs. What she has is awareness: she knows how many creatures are in her cavern at any moment, she remembers what hurt her and what didn't, she adapts her approach between encounters, and she uses her brood as extensions of her senses. By the time the party reaches her chamber, she knows exactly how many of them there are, which direction they came from, and roughly how dangerous they are. She has been preparing since they entered. She is not angry. She is efficient.
Getting Down
Read Aloud — The Quiet Docks, Access Hatch
The hatch is iron, set into the dock's lowest platform, above the waterline by a handspan. It opens onto a ladder descending into dark and the smell of cold stone and river damp. Someone has been here recently — the hatch's locking bar is drawn back and there are scuff marks on the rungs. At the bottom of the ladder, the light from above reaches just far enough to show you a passage heading east into the rock. Then it stops reaching.
GM Note — The Access Ladder
The ladder descends 40 feet into the first passage. It is in reasonable condition — the smugglers maintained it. A DC 14 Perception check on the way down notices a thin thread of silk crossing the passage at chest height, approximately 20 feet below the hatch. It is not a tripwire in a mechanical sense. It is a web sensor — disturbing it alerts the Queen to a new entry. The party almost certainly triggers it. That's fine. She already knows they're coming from the moment they opened the hatch — the vibrations in the dock structure carry to her web network. The sensor just confirms numbers.
GM Note — Light and Sound
The upper passages are dark except for faint bioluminescent spore-glow filtering from the main cavern ahead. The party will need light sources unless they have darkvision. Sound carries unusually well in the stone passages — a dropped object in the upper warehouse can be heard in the deep cavern as a faint tap. The Queen uses this. If the party is loud, encounters in the next chamber will have her brood pre-positioned. If they're quiet, they buy themselves a round of surprise. Tell them this through the environment: their footsteps sound too loud to them. They're right.
Upper Cavern — Smuggler Territory

The upper sections of the cavern were in active use until three nights ago. The smugglers were competent people running a modest operation, and the remnants of their work are still here — along with evidence of what happened to them.

Deep Cavern — The Queen's Territory

Past the main cavern ceiling the environment changes. The bioluminescent ceiling thickens to a carpet of glowing spores — the Queen's egg clusters, slowly pulsing. The stonework here is older, pre-smuggler, and carved rather than natural. Something was here before her. She moved into an existing structure.

The Venom Queen
Read Aloud — First Sight
The ceiling of the final chamber is entirely alive. Every surface above you moves with the slow pulse of something breathing — egg clusters, hundreds of them, glowing violet-white in a light that makes everything below look like it's underwater. At the center of the chamber, suspended in a web that spans wall to wall like the floor of a second room above yours, she hangs. Eight eyes. Each one separately focused. She has been watching you since you entered the cavern. She makes no move toward you. She is simply letting you understand the situation before it begins.

On round 2 and beyond, she gains a +2 circumstance bonus to saves against any spell or ability that successfully targeted her in a previous round. She adjusts positioning to minimize exposure to attack types that have damaged her most.
  • Egg Cluster Destruction Each egg cluster destroyed (AC 14, HP 12, immune to piercing) causes the Queen to take 2d6 psychic damage and lose her reaction until the start of her next turn. She will immediately prioritize the creature that destroyed it. This is not rational. It is the one thing that overrides her tactical awareness.
  • Web Platform The Queen's web spans the upper half of the chamber. Creatures on the web treat it as difficult terrain. The Queen ignores this. Webs can be cut (DC 20 Athletics) or burned (fire damage drops sections). A fully burning web section forces the Queen to relocate and deals 3d6 fire damage to her.
GM Note — Running the Queen
She opens by throwing web at whoever looks most dangerous — the heaviest armor, the one holding the biggest weapon. She will not drop from the ceiling immediately; she will observe for one round if the party hesitates, using that time to position for a Ceiling Drop on whoever moves first. She uses Brood Call only when she is taking significant damage, not as an opener — she doesn't need the help immediately and conserves it. She targets isolated characters preferentially. If a character destroys an egg cluster, all of that tactical patience evaporates — she will pursue that character single-mindedly for 1d3 rounds before her awareness reasserts. This is the lever the party can pull if they're struggling.
GM Note — Retreat and Pursuit
The Queen will not leave her egg chamber voluntarily. If the party retreats, she lets them go — they are no longer in her territory. She will, however, completely reseal the passage with fresh webs within the hour. A retreating party can rest outside the cavern system and return; the encounter will reset her HP but not her Adaptive Memory. She remembers what worked on her. She will position differently the second time.
People Involved
What Happens After

The cavern system doesn't stop being a problem when the Queen is dead. It stops being an immediate problem. Everything else remains.

If the Queen is Killed
The egg clusters survive her death and will hatch in approximately three weeks without her to regulate them. The juvenile spiders will be individually manageable but collectively a significant infestation problem beneath Millhaven. Someone needs to deal with the eggs. This is either a follow-up contract or a problem Aldric quietly asks the party to handle "since you were already down there." The cavern itself is now a known, cleared space — and Sable Rei will immediately recognize its potential as a far better base of operations than the Quiet Docks. Whether the Guild knows about this use is a question for later.
If the Queen is Driven Off (Retreated Below)
The deeper passages continue below the egg chamber — the map shows only the upper system. She retreats into whatever is further down, which is uncharted. The upper cavern is safe. The question of what is below it remains open. This is a hook for a later, deeper delve at higher level.
The Documents
The cargo crate is in the warehouse room, buried under collapsed shelving from the initial panic. The documents inside are water-damaged but legible. They are exactly as politically dangerous as Sable Rei implied — correspondence implicating a Legea Empire trade official in smuggling operations that technically constitute economic warfare against the Nanuu Republic. Sable Rei will pay in full and ask no questions about whether the party read them. The party almost certainly read them. What they do with that knowledge is their business. It is, however, notable that one of the letters references a contact in Millhaven by title rather than name: "our friend on the bridge council."
The Pre-Smuggler Stonework
The carved stone in the deep cavern predates the smuggler operation by centuries — possibly by much more. A History check in the statue room puts it contemporaneous with the late Golden Empire or earlier. The statues depict figures that don't match any known culture's aesthetic. Professor Odo Mast, if the party rescued him from the Veldtmark estate, will be intensely interested in rubbings of the stonework and will connect them, eventually, to something he found in a very old text. That connection is a later thread. Plant it now.
The Bridge Council Contact
The letter references "our friend on the bridge council." There are three bridge council members: Aldric Fenn, Sable Rei, and Tomas Hewer. Sable Rei posted the contract and therefore either doesn't know about this letter or wants the party to find it for reasons of their own. Aldric is Aldric. That leaves Tomas Hewer, the legitimately stressed legitimate businessman — or it's a misdirect and it's Sable Rei after all, using the guild to retrieve incriminating evidence before anyone else finds it. Leave this unresolved. Let the party argue about it.
