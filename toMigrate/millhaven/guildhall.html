The Building

The Wayward Compass has occupied its spot on the central bridge since 1,891 MR — over six hundred years, through the tail end of the Dark Era and everything that came after. It did not start as a guildhall. It started as a storage shed that a group of adventurers rented by the week, then couldn't stop renting because it was convenient, and then quietly bought when the owner died without heirs.

It has grown the way the bridge district itself has grown — upward first, then outward, then eventually downward onto pilings that were originally just meant to support a practice post for Kael's sparring sessions. The original shed is now somewhere inside the walls of the second floor. Nobody is entirely sure which walls. Rumor has it, it still houses an artifact of the original trio who founded this hall. Aldric will scowl if you go looking for it.

From the bridge level, it presents a respectable face: a wide door, the compass rose sign, Mira Seld's desk visible through the front window. From the river, it looks like something that decided it wanted to be a boat but couldn't commit. Ropes run between the piling extensions and the bridge above. The sound of the river is constant — louder in the lower levels, a soft undertone in the upper floors. Regulars stop noticing it. New arrivals find it either calming or unsettling.

What Makes It Feel Like Home
Maps cover almost every wall — some current, some historical, some of places nobody has been able to identify for decades. Trophy cases line the drinking hall stairs: a basilisk scale, a piece of what might be Elden stonework, three weapons of unusual make with small plaques listing the names of the people who brought them back. The plaques list when they were recovered. They do not always list what happened to the person who recovered them. Regulars know which ones to avoid reading.
