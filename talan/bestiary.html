Bestiary & Ancestries
The Playable Peoples of Talan & the Beings Adjacent to Them
A reference for who lives where, which heritages reach which veins, and what the demons and devils of Talan actually are. Mechanical rules live in Pathfinder 2e Remaster; this page is the where-and-why.
Standard ancestries
44 PF2e Remaster ancestries placed across the thirteen domains; six more native to Sortalde.
Tyrnarra-canon
Three additions outside the standard roster: Dragons, Slimes, Rabbitfolk.
Sortalde petals
Six ancestries, one per outer/inner petal of the eastern continent. All rare on Talan. See Sortalde →
Heritages
Six divine-blood lines, five elemental-plane sparks, two Life-Layer planar lines (Wood / Metal), two mortal-mix lines, four other lineages.
Demons & devils
Virtue demons follow an Aristotelian excess/deficiency pattern; sin devils are the seven sins given form.

Domain by Domain

Where each domain's dominant ancestries live, what character they share with their god, and the cultural inflection that makes each people of that land.

Egulon · Light
Iro · sun-and-radiance

Nagaji Iro's most fervent worshippers — the public face of his church, almost zealous in their certainty.

Shoony All dog-coded ancestries fold here; loyal and devoted, aligned with the Nagaji-led religious life.

Myrkono · Darkness
Araphel · new faces, second chances

Fetchling Remnants of a civilisation that sought refuge in the region of Darkness and found it. Most live in Myrria.

Drow Surface-dwelling children of shadow — not the underdark stereotype. Talan's underdark is the Darklands, and they are not Araphel's.

Centaur (Shadow Steppes) Smaller herds than their Villtur cousins, more nocturnal, more theologically inflected.

Sumendar · Fire
Komo · forge, flame, invention

Dragons An alien race; their mothership crash-landed in the Gods' Era. Capital at Dragon's Reach. Modern goal: purity — recovering their pre-corruption nature.

Dwarf Two cultures — New Age in the Order of Steam, and Sentinel deep in the upper Darklands.

Kobold Genius chaotic inventors who don't care about safety. Komo himself is depicted as a kobold.

Floteyn · Water
Shuun · sea, spray, drifting isles

Strix Flight suits the islet-archipelago life. Suspect almost everywhere else.

Tripkee The most settled of the islet four — they build where the others pass through, swim past, or pass underneath.

Athamaru Seal-folk of the Floating Isles and the Hafra coast; amphibious by habit.

Merfolk Coastal and Hafra waters; a small freshwater pod lives in Midarra with informal Twin Cities alliance.

Brauogi · Earth
Sarrum · stone, soil, patience

Hobgoblin & Goblin Share the Goblin & Hobgoblin Kingdom — the major political sub-region of Brauogi.

Minotaur Sarrum is depicted as a Minotaur. Immovable, patient, slow to forgive; excellent stonecutters and contract negotiators.

Brauogi's three-way is cooperative — the Earth ancestries trade rather than feud.

Vindul · Wind
Fisaya · sky, storm, monastery

Kashrishi Secular by inclination — theology private, civic life public. Inverse of Egulon's zeal.

Tengu Often become Monks; Vindul's mountain monasticism produces a steady stream of wanderers.

Jotunborn (Baerfrost) Frost-adapted, cold-hardy, often nomadic; the largest stable population on Talan.

Lioaru · Time
Tani · slow currents, vigilance

Ghoran "Guardians of Time" — explicitly aligned with Tani's domain; many warden the temporally unstable sites along the Lost Kingdom border.

Azarketi Cluster on Lioaru's western coast and the islands offshore of the Lost Kingdom. Inherited folklore still claims Elden blood — a long-distorted memory.

GM Truth
Azarketi descend from the experimented-upon population of the Storveldi Denbora — the mortal civilisation whose ascended ruling class killed Tani and triggered the Week of Crimson Rain. Their inherited "Elden blood" claim is the original Storveldi lie, transmitted forward across millennia. Any Azarketi character who follows the bloodline far enough back hits the integration procedure that elevated mortals past Demi-God status. See the GM Secrets on Lost Kingdom, history, and Magic & Faith.
Zuzental · Law
Forseti · contract, custody, order

Elf Politically motivated and long-planning — moves on generational timescales. One of the two ruling bloodlines of the Thousand Kingdom.

Human Politically motivated and short-planning — moves fast, takes risks, burns through cycles. The other ruling bloodline.

The interplay between elven slow and human fast is what gives the Thousand Kingdom its volatility — and its longevity.

Ehizahar · Hunt
Hinka · pursuit, blood-token, pack

The four-way of the Lands of Villtur — Orc, Kholo, Lizardfolk, Centaur — intermix and feud along recognisable patterns.

Orc Loudest, first to charge, most consistently in feud. Hinka is depicted as a red-headed orc.

Kholo Most explicitly matriarchal of the four; bone-token-marked.

Lizardfolk Most strategic — slower to start, more patient in pursuit, more deliberate at the kill.

Centaur (Villtur) The largest centaur population on Talan; tribal and hunt-aligned.

Lautara · Commerce
Jianna · road, market, contract-of-trust

Halfling Wanderlust-driven — the call of the next market is doctrinally central. Carry more halflings than any other domain.

Vishkanya Community-keeping and creative-pursuing. Over-represented in artisan-merchant guilds. Jianna herself is depicted as a vishkanya.

Kitsune (Emarrea) Concentrated in Emarrea, the kitsune kingdom — Heartcourt government, capital at Biozuri.

Ezkudon · Knowledge
Enki · archive, negotiation, secret-keeping

Catfolk Curious; over-represented in the scholar-caste and archive-keeper professions. Enki is depicted as a black-haired catfolk.

Shisk Hunters of esoteric knowledge — shisk-curiosity is a common warning given to junior novices.

Nashavel · Chaos
Vesuna · improvisation, free will, the Feyworld at the edges

Gnome Feyworld-native; long migrated into the Material Plane during the Gods' Era and after.

Vanara Common in the Basogur Jungle straddling Nashavel and Ehizahar — one of the few cultures comfortable across two domains.

Conrasu · Anadi Uncommon here, rare elsewhere. Matron Charna of the Spider's Silk Inn is Talan's most famous Anadi.

Askamira · Freedom
Cronus · refusal, refuge, the open road

All ancestries equally. Askamira holds no demographic majority by design — Cronus's domain is the one place on Talan where origin counts for nothing and the choice you arrive with counts for everything.

Without a Single Home

Ancestries with no domain heartland — either scattered, refugee, made, or simply not numerous enough to dominate anywhere.

  • Rabbitfolk Widely scattered, frequently nomadic, no continental-scale institutions of their own.
  • Ratfolk Originally from the Darklands; now thrive as the most adaptable urban underclass in every city. Often serve as guides for Adventurers' Guild parties heading underground.
  • Dragonet Indigenous wyrmkin — children of Zaharsuge, predating the alien Dragons by ages. Lean toward indigenous wyrm-cultism; many reject Dragon's Reach's authority claim on theological grounds.
  • Surki Insectoid sapients of the upper Darklands; share the territory loosely with the Sentinel Dwarves. Antagonistic to the Vermin Queen by design.
  • Sprite Native to the Feyworld; rare on the Material Plane. They go where they go.
  • Slimes Uncommon in the Darklands' ambient corruption pressure; surface Slimes almost always worked their way up.
  • Awakened Animal No native population — each is its own story, typically traced to a druid awakening, ambient Komo-flame-touch, or Feyworld leakage.
  • Poppet Constructed beings; rare; no domain-specific concentration.
  • Android Synthetic people created by the Elden; declining for millennia, very few remain. The Golden Empire's attempt to recreate them produced Automatons instead.
  • Automaton The Golden Empire's would-be Androids — capable, formidable, not the same thing. Now concentrated in Sumendar where Dwarves and Kobolds slowly recover the maintenance technology.
  • Fleshwarp Native to the Blackened Lands (the canonical lineage), or fabricated by the Red Empire's Menagerie. Same end-product, completely different origin.
  • Skeleton Native to the Blackened Lands — the wound in the land pulls the dead back into motion. Spiritually tied to Betibizi, the Minor God of Undeath.
  • Azarketi Dominant in Lioaru's western coast; aquatic adaptations cluster their communities along Hafra-side trade routes. Inherited folklore claims Elden blood.

Sortalde — The Six Petals

Six ancestries native to Sortalde (Tao Hua Yuan internally), the petal-archipelago continent east of Talan across Hafra. Each has one ancestral homeland petal; the seventh — Heting, the Concord-Court — is the central seat where all six meet through their ambassadors.

On Talan, all six are rare. The crossing is hard, and most arrivals come through the standing Sortalde embassies on the Emerald Isles' Bridgelands — the cloudship landing point for Sortalde traffic. A Sortalde-ancestry character on Talan is a story, not a demographic.

YaoguaiWandao (Myriad Island) — outer petalMost-likely-seen; closest petal to Talan. Yaoguai merchants occasionally settle in Lautara.
TanukiXidao (Theatre Island) — outer petalItinerant performer-merchants; the largest standing population on Talan is around the embassies.
SarangayNiudao (Ox Island) — outer petalUsually attached to embassy security details. Their oaths survive reincarnation cycles.
WayangYingdao (Shadow Island) — inner petalVanishingly rare; those who cross are typically working — embedded spies or freelance information brokers.
YakshaLingdao (Spirit Island) — inner petalFunctionally never. Bound to specific places; a Talan-side Yaksha is doctrinally an exile whose bond was broken.
SamsaranLundao (Wheel Island) — inner petalVanishingly rare. A Talan-side Samsaran is almost always a retired chancellor or a heretic.

Versatile Heritages

The PF2e Remaster reshuffled the versatile heritage roster — Aasimar and Tiefling folded into Nephilim; Wood and Metal added as Ardande and Talos; new ancestry-mixes (Aiuvarin, Dromaar). The placement below is the post-remaster canon for Talan: most heritages are explained in-world as a god's blood, an Elemental Plane spark, a Life-Layer planar lineage, or another non-standard origin.

Nephilim — the universal heritage
Remaster Nephilim covers both celestial and fiendish features. On Talan that broadens further: celestial features can manifest in Nephilim descended from any god — most commonly Iro (Light), but any of the bound thirteen, and sometimes banned/expelled gods too. Fiendish features manifest in Nephilim descended from any virtue demon or sin devil (see the demon and devil tables below). Two Nephilim from different parent lineages may share nothing beyond the divinely-touched flag — one glows faintly, another smells of brimstone, another casts a shadow with three more limbs than the body has. Identifying the parent line is a skill — and a service Enki's scholars sometimes sell.

These heritages carry the spark of the Elemental Plane itself. The Primordial Six who created Prelife include the four bound elemental gods — so each elemental-heritage individual has a doctrinal affinity with their corresponding god, but the lineage is properly elemental-plane, not divine blood. Suli is the exception: generic geniekin without a single-element specialisation.

A common confusion
Neither plane belongs to a bound god. The thirteen all reside on the Material Plane; the Feyworld and Shadowplane are sibling planes that no god owns. Ardande are not Vesuna's by blood — Vesuna is god of Chaos but her domain is Nashavel on the Material, not the Feyworld. Talos are not Araphel-aligned despite the verbal overlap — the Shadowplane is order, not darkness, and Forseti's domain is Zuzental, not the Shadowplane. Both lineages live without a god-claimant.

Virtue Demons — The Aristotelian Pattern

Talan's demons follow a coherent theological pattern that mortal scholars have catalogued: each demon is the personification of a virtue's corruption. Following the classical doctrine of the mean, every virtue sits between two vices — one of excess, one of deficiency. A demon embodies one of those two extremes.

This is why the demons are not random or symmetric: they are exactly as numerous as there are virtue/extreme pairs in the Talanese moral tradition, and they map cleanly to those pairs.

Named so far · Muiral the Misshapen
The only individually named virtue demon in canon. Muiral is a Modesty-line demon (Shamelessness / Shyness pair) and grants the Abomination domain — corrupted Modesty either dissolves form (Shyness extreme) or inflates it into grotesque self-display (Shamelessness extreme); either produces the misshapen. Muiral appears in mortal exorcism texts and in a handful of Adventurers' Guild high-rank contract reports. The rest of the virtue lines have demons too — they just have not yet named themselves.

Implications. Demons of opposed extremes within the same virtue line are theological enemies as well as cosmic ones — the Cowardice-demon and the Rashness-demon both pervert Courage, and both have reasons to hate each other. Mortal philosophers who study the pattern can predict demon behaviour; a handful of Enki-aligned exorcists work by reading the demon, identifying its line, then deploying the opposite extreme to push it off-pattern.

Sin Devils — The Seven

The devils of Talan are organised on a simpler pattern: the Seven Sins. Unlike the virtue demons (corruption of something good), sin devils embody the sins themselves directly. One canonical devil-lord per sin — seven sins, seven lords, no more, no less.

Three are bound to grantable domains via the Domains Outside the Thirteen mapping. The other four grantable domains, and all seven devil-lord names, remain open.

Demon & Devil — the Talan distinction
Demons corrupt a virtue. Their existence is parasitic on the virtue line — perversions of what could have been good. Devils embody a sin. Their existence is foundational — the sin given form. Both are redeemable in principle — the virtue line still runs through every demon, and every devil retains the seed of the contrary virtue. Redemption work is rare and brutally difficult, but it is canonically possible — no being on Talan is fixed beyond change. Demons proliferate around mortals who have already half-corrupted a virtue; devils proliferate around mortals who choose a sin.
