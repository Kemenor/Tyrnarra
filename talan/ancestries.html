Ancestries
The Peoples of Talan & the Lineages Walking Among Them
A chronicler's account of who lives where on the continent, which heritages reach which veins, and how the lineages of the world shape its kingdoms and its faiths.
Peoples of Talan
The continent holds many peoples, distributed across the thirteen domains by ancient pattern of culture, climate, and kinship. The Dragons of Sumendar are an alien race whose mothership crash-landed in the Gods' Era; all other peoples are native.
Sortalde petals
Six further peoples are native to Sortalde, the petal-archipelago east across the Cloud Sea. All six are rare on Talan. See Sortalde →
Heritages
Lineages that carry something other than ordinary ancestry: divine blood (god in the veins), elemental sparks from the Prelife layer, Life-Layer planar lines (Wood & Metal, from the Feyworld and Shadowplane), mortal-ancestry mixes, and a small handful of other unique lines.
Lifespan
No mortal of Talan is known to have lived past two and a half centuries. This shapes politics, warfare, and theology across the continent: dynasties move quickly, scholars work in lineages, and no one mortal sees a century of history through their own eyes alone. Gods and the undead step outside this limit.

Domain by Domain

Where each domain's peoples make their home, the character they share with their god, and the cultural inflection that makes each people of that land.

Egulon · Light →
Iro · sun-and-radiance

Nagaji Iro's most fervent worshippers, the public face of his church, almost zealous in their certainty.

Shoony Every dog-shaped people lives among the Shoony; loyal and devoted, aligned with the Nagaji-led religious life.
Myrkono · Darkness →
Araphel · new faces, second chances

Fetchling Dominant ancestry of Myrkono. Remnants of a civilisation that sought refuge in the region of Darkness and found it; most live in Myrria.

Centaur (Shadow Steppes) Smaller herds than their Villtur cousins, more nocturnal, more theologically inflected.
Sumendar · Fire →
Komo · forge, flame, invention

Dragons An alien race; their mothership crash-landed in the Gods' Era. Capital at Dragon's Reach. Modern goal: purity: recovering their pre-corruption nature.

Dwarf Two cultures: New Age in the Order of Steam, and Sentinel deep in the upper Darklands.

Kobold Genius chaotic inventors who don't care about safety. Komo himself is depicted as a kobold.
Floteyn · Water →
Shuun · sea, spray, drifting isles

Tripkee The most settled of the islet three, they build where the others swim past or pass underneath.

Athamaru Seal-folk of the Drifting Islands and the Hafra coast; amphibious by habit.

Merfolk Coastal and Hafra waters; a small freshwater pod lives in Midarra with informal Twin Cities alliance.
Brauogi · Earth →
Sarrum · stone, soil, patience

Hobgoblin & Goblin Share the Goblin & Hobgoblin Kingdom, the major political sub-region of Brauogi.

Minotaur Sarrum is depicted as a Minotaur. Immovable, patient, slow to forgive; excellent stonecutters and contract negotiators.

Brauogi's three-way is cooperative, the Earth ancestries trade rather than feud.
Vindul · Wind →
Fisaya · sky, storm, monastery

Kashrishi Secular by inclination, theology private, civic life public. Inverse of Egulon's zeal. Anchor ancestry of the Wyndwalken cartographer order at the Air Monastery.

Tengu Often become Monks; Vindul's mountain monasticism produces a steady stream of wanderers.

Strix (Fellibylur) Storm-people of the Vindul steppe and Hafra coast; winged sky-keepers of weather-lore. Nomadic interior, port-dwelling coast; core of Fellibylur's elite Stormriders.

Jotunborn (Baerfrost) Frost-adapted, cold-hardy, often nomadic; the largest stable population on Talan.
Lioaru · Time →
Tani · slow currents, vigilance

Ghoran "Guardians of Time", explicitly aligned with Tani's domain; many warden the temporally unstable sites along the Lost Kingdom border.

Azarketi Cluster on Lioaru's southern coast and the islands offshore of the Lost Kingdom. Inherited folklore still claims Elden blood: a long-distorted memory.
Zuzental · Law →
Forseti · contract, custody, order

Elf Politically motivated and long-planning: moves on generational timescales. One of the two ruling bloodlines of the Thousand Kingdom.

Human Politically motivated and short-planning: moves fast, takes risks, burns through cycles. The other ruling bloodline.

The interplay between elven slow and human fast is what gives the Thousand Kingdom its volatility, and its longevity.
Ehizahar · Hunt →
Hinka · pursuit, blood-token, pack

The four-way of the Lands of Villtur: Orc, Kholo, Lizardfolk, Centaur, intermix and feud along recognisable patterns.

Orc Loudest, first to charge, most consistently in feud. Hinka is depicted as a red-headed orc.

Kholo Most explicitly matriarchal of the four; bone-token-marked.

Lizardfolk Most strategic, slower to start, more patient in pursuit, more deliberate at the kill.

Centaur (Villtur) The largest centaur population on Talan; tribal and hunt-aligned.
Lautara · Commerce →
Jianna · road, market, contract-of-trust

Halfling Wanderlust-driven; the call of the next market is doctrinally central. Lautara is home to more halflings than any other domain.

Vishkanya Community-keeping and creative-pursuing; well-represented in artisan-merchant guilds. Jianna herself is depicted as a vishkanya.

Kitsune The third people of Lautara's heartland, concentrated in Emarrea, the kitsune kingdom in Lautara's forested western highlands.
Ezkudon · Knowledge →
Enki · archive, negotiation, secret-keeping

Catfolk Curious; over-represented in the scholar-caste and archive-keeper professions. Enki is depicted as a black-haired catfolk.

Shisk Hunters of esoteric knowledge: shisk-curiosity is a common warning given to junior novices.
Nashavel · Chaos →
Vesuna · improvisation, free will, the Feyworld at the edges

Gnome Feyworld-native; long migrated into the Material Plane during the Gods' Era and after.

Vanara Common in the Basogur Jungle straddling Nashavel and Ehizahar, one of the few cultures comfortable across two domains.

Conrasu · Anadi Uncommon here, rare elsewhere. Matron Charna of the Spider's Silk Inn is Talan's most famous Anadi.
Askamira · Freedom →
Cronus · refusal, refuge, the open road

All ancestries equally. Askamira holds no demographic majority by design, Cronus's domain is the one place on Talan where origin counts for nothing and the choice you arrive with counts for everything.

Without a Homeland

Peoples who have no single domain to call home. Some are scattered, some are refugee, some were made rather than born, some are simply too few to dominate any land.

  • Ratfolk Originally from the Darklands; now thrive as the most adaptable urban underclass in every city. Often serve as guides for Adventurers' Guild parties heading underground.
  • Dragonet Indigenous wyrmkin, children of Zaharsuge, predating the alien Dragons by ages. Lean toward indigenous wyrm-cultism; many reject Dragon's Reach's authority claim on theological grounds.
  • Surki Insectoid sapients of the upper Darklands; share the territory loosely with the Sentinel Dwarves. Antagonistic to the Vermin Queen by design.
  • Sprite Native to the Feyworld; rare on the Material Plane. They go where they go.
  • Awakened Animal No native population, each is its own story, typically traced to a druid awakening, ambient Komo-flame-touch, or Feyworld leakage.
  • Poppet Constructed beings; rare; no domain-specific concentration.
  • Android Synthetic people created by the Elden; declining for millennia, very few remain. The Golden Empire's attempt to recreate them produced Automatons instead.
  • Automaton The Golden Empire's would-be Androids, capable, formidable, not the same thing. Now concentrated in Sumendar where Dwarves and Kobolds slowly recover the maintenance technology.
  • Fleshwarp Native to the Blackened Lands (the canonical lineage), or fabricated by the Red Empire's Menagerie. Same end-product, completely different origin.
  • Skeleton Native to the Blackened Lands, the wound in the land pulls the dead back into motion. Spiritually tied to Betibizi, the Minor God of Undeath.

Sortalde · The Six Petals

Six ancestries native to Sortalde (Tao Hua Yuan internally), the petal-archipelago continent east of Talan.

On Talan, all six are rare. The crossing is hard, and most arrivals come through the standing Sortalde embassies on the Emerald Isles' Bridgelands, the cloudship landing point for Sortalde traffic. A Sortalde-ancestry character on Talan is a story, not a demographic.

SarangayNiudao (Ox Island), outer petalUsually attached to embassy security details. Their oaths survive reincarnation cycles.

Heritages

Some mortals are born carrying something more than their parents' blood: a god's touch in the veins, a spark from one of the elemental currents of Prelife, a strain of the Life-Layer's twin sibling planes, a parentage of two mortal peoples whose lines mix, or rarer origins still. Each lineage is visible, often striking, and met with its own reception. Some are blessed, some are feared, most are simply known and lived around. The lines below are grouped by where the touch comes from. Nephilim is the universal divine-blood (and fiendish-blood) lineage; see the dedicated callout below the divine table.

Nephilim · the universal lineage
Nephilim on Talan carry both celestial and fiendish marks. Celestial marks can manifest in Nephilim descended from any god, most commonly Iro (Light) but any of the bound thirteen, and sometimes from gods now withdrawn beyond the veil. Fiendish marks manifest in Nephilim descended from any Vice Demon or Virtue Devil. Two Nephilim from different parent lineages may share nothing beyond the divine touch itself: one glows faintly, another smells of brimstone, another casts a shadow with three more limbs than the body has. Reading the parent line takes a practised eye, and is a service Enki's scholars sometimes sell.

These lines carry the spark of the Elemental Plane itself. The Primordial Six who shaped Prelife include the four bound elemental gods, so each Elemental-touched mortal has a doctrinal kinship with their corresponding god; the bloodline, though, runs to the plane rather than to the deity. Suli stand outside the four pure elements, mixed in their spark and at home in none.

How the planar lines run
The Feyworld and Shadowplane are the sibling planes of the Life Layer, standing without a god-claimant; all thirteen of the bound gods reside on the Material Plane. Ardande carry the Feyworld's living-growth element; their lineage runs to the Feyworld itself, alongside the bound thirteen rather than through them. Vesuna is god of Chaos on the Material, with her domain in Nashavel; Ardande gather by cultural gravity wherever their kin are welcomed. Talos carry the Shadowplane's eternal-structure element. The plane is order, and Talos express that order rather than the surface-verbal overlap with darkness. Their lineage runs to the Shadowplane itself, alongside the bound thirteen; Talos communities form around craft and structure, gathering by trade.

Children of two peoples whose lines mix. Their place in the world is shaped not by a god or a plane but by the politics of the two parent peoples and how those politics frame the child of both.
