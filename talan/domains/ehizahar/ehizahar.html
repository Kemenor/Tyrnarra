At a Glance

Etymology
Basque ehiza (hunt) + zahar (ancient, old) → naturally eroded. The ancient hunt. The oldest name on Talan.
Position
Northeast Talan: the largest domain, spanning arctic north to jungle south
Terrain
The most varied domain. Arctic/icy tundra in the far north, with a small ice-isle archipelago off the northern coast. The highest mountain range on the continent and the oldest trees (ancient dark conifers, northwest corner). Open grassland through the centre. Deep jungle-like old-growth forest in the south and southeast. Rivers throughout.
Character
Ancient forest, arctic tundra, jungle

Sub-Regions

Lands of Villtur
Icelandic villtur (wild, lost, strayed)
The umbrella territory covering most of Ehizahar. Overwhelmingly tribal: no unified kingdoms, no formal borders. The many tribes that live across the arctic tundra, grasslands, and ancient forests fall under this collective name. Veidrath sits within this expanse.

Ardo Beroa
Basque ardo (wine) + beroa (hot/warm), "warm wine"
The small ice-isle archipelago off Ehizahar's northern arctic coast. The name's literal meaning is at ironic odds with the geography: drift has partly obscured the etymology, and the locals lean into the joke.
