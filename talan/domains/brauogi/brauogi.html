Brauogi
Earth Domain
Bound to Sarrum
Where the source-lake feeds the rivers, the granaries fill, and the northern half eats.
✦

At a Glance

Etymology
Icelandic brauð (bread) + Basque ogi (bread) → fused to Brauogi. Both roots mean bread; the breadbasket named twice over in two ancient tongues.
Position
Northwest-central Talan, directly south of Vindul. Neighbours: Vindul (north), Ehizahar (east), Midarra (east/southeast), Floteyn (southeast corner), Myrkono (south); Hafra coast to the west. Twin Suns and Soul Tree sit on the Midarra-touching corner.
Terrain
Rolling fertile lowlands cut by dense river networks; most of the domain's rivers spring from Iturmen, the central east-west mountain spine, with their headwaters in Iturburu, the great mountain-lake under Iturmen's tallest peak. Eraztumen, the ring of peaks around Lurrath, gives Gotorlekua its Stronghold character. Mugamen, the southern range, walls Brauogi off from Myrkono's deep forests. Western dark-mountains shelter the domain from weather rolling in off the Hafra coast. Agricultural heartland in earth-tones throughout: deep greens, brown river-soil, grey-and-rust mountain-stone, the gold of late-summer fields.
Character
The steady counterweight. Source-lake, grain-belt, mountain-ring, orchard-shore.
BreadbasketEarth DomainNorthern Larder
Cultural register
Earth-domain, Sarrum-the-Steadfast at its centre. Old noble houses control river trade and the granaries; the three-way ancestry politics (Goblin / Hobgoblin / Minotaur) is more cooperative than feud-prone, because the rivers and roads have always been worth more to share than to fight over. The breadbasket feeds Vindul, Ehizahar, Myrkono, and Floteyn through nearby supply lines; the south-toward-Lautara grain trade runs across Midarra. The domain reads as the steady counterweight to its neighbours' more dramatic registers: Vindul's storms, Myrkono's shadow, Ehizahar's wild, Floteyn's water.
Peoples
Three dominant ancestries are anchored here: Goblin, Hobgoblin, and Minotaur. Sarrum himself is depicted as a Minotaur, and Brauogi's Minotaurs are his cultural cousins. The three-way of Earth-domain politics is more cooperative than feud-prone; they trade rather than fight.
Bound God
Sarrum, domain of Earth
Magitrain
On the northern continental network. Confederated multi-operator system; no single commercial heart-city at present, the northern network runs as a distributed multi-node system. Regional lines through the river valleys link Brauogi to Vindul, Myrkono, and Ehizahar. A future consolidation around Lurrath is plausible but currently absent.
God's City
Lurrath
The Steadfast City

Lurrath sits within Eraztumen (Basque eraztun "ring" + mendi "mountain"), the ring of peaks that gives Gotorlekua its Stronghold name. The inside of the ring is the sub-region proper; the ring itself is the natural border. The city and the sub-region grew together inside the mountain ring.

At the centre of the domain, Iturmen (the source-mountain spine) and Iturburu (the source-lake under its tallest peak) are unclaimed natural territory. The headwaters of most of Brauogi's rivers spring from Iturburu; the pass-towns on the spine answer to themselves; the lake and its peaks are tended by druids attached to Hirubaso. Where Lautara has the mythic-pilgrim Helgafjall above Merkavar's lake, Brauogi has the practical-livelihood Iturburu — the same geometric shape in opposite register.

Azkamour
Basque azkar (swift) + muga (border): "the Swift March"
Border territory straddling Vindul and Brauogi, capital on the Vindul side. Mostly golden Brauogi-character grain territory inland, but the Vindul coastal stretch anchors the capital where storm-fishing meets grain-roads; the fast riders carry both ways.

Gotorlekua

Houses Lurrath, Sarrum's city-state, set within the ring of peaks called Eraztumen that gives the sub-region its Stronghold character. The defensible interior heart of the domain; the city-state and the sub-region grew together inside the mountain ring.

The Earth Realm
Modern English
The agricultural heart of Brauogi between Iturmen (north) and Mugamen (south); the broad fertile plains of the breadbasket. Fully golden plains — grain, livestock, market-towns at every road-fork, the daily-bread of the northern domains. Hirubaso sits as an enclave in its south-centre but is its own independent sub-region (see below).
BreadbasketGolden Plains
Hirubaso
Basque hiru (three) + baso (forest); drifted from hiru-ibai-baso "three-river-forest" via loss of medial ibai
An independent sub-region in the south-centre, the sacred druid grove of Sarrum where three rivers converge in the open plains. Tended by an order of Sarrum's faithful; the still point of the breadbasket, the wild left untouched at the heart of the cultivated world. The only routine Primotech site on the continent, where Brauogi's legendary mineral-Magitech tradition finds its grown-not-built source; the rumoured Brauogi heart-stone is grown here.

Baratalda
Basque baratze (garden, orchard) + aldea (region); drift drops the medial z
The eastern arm of Brauogi at the Ehizahar border. Mixed golden plain and green orchard country; the orchard-and-fruit-cellar of the domain where the harvest-fairs draw Ehizahar hunters down from the Lands of Villtur to trade game and furs for wine, apple-brandy, and stone-fruit. The frontier-trade kingdom of the bread-domain.

Twin Suns
Modern English · named for a phenomenon native to the sub-region
Coastal sub-region on the Midarra-touching corner, the Brauogi-side half of the Twin Suns / Bikitsa border kingdom shared with Myrkono. Golden, food-rich coast where the two-sun phenomenon is observed at certain seasons. The Myrkono-side counterpart (Bikitsa, Basque biki-itzal "twin-shadow") sees the same phenomenon from its shadow-domain side; the two halves are jointly governed under a single shared structure (form canon-pending). The specific phenomenon that earned the name is canon-pending.

Soul Tree
Modern English
A small rocky island in Midarra, just off the coast of Twin Suns. Named for the single massive pale tree at its centre, an actual tree, through which souls may wander on to the Postlife. One of the most cosmologically significant sites on Talan: a natural passage point between the Life Layer and the Postlife. The Voroir Daua's oldest charter sits directly on the island; the cycle-warden order's permanent presence here predates all its other charters. A great rooted tree through which souls pass is Sarrum-the-Steadfast iconography; the Earth-domain affiliation reads as the cosmological resonance behind the sub-region's Brauogi attribution.
