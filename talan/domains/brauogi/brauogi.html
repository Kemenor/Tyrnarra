At a Glance

Etymology
Icelandic brauð (bread) + Basque ogi (bread) → fused. The breadbasket named twice over.
Position
South-central Talan, north of Lioaru, south of Midarra, bordered by Sumendar to the west and Ezkudon/Egulon to the east
Terrain
Wide, green, fertile — Talan's breadbasket. Rolling hills and river valleys dominant. Multiple mountain ranges but not harsh. Dense forest in the south. River network throughout. Agricultural heartland with old noble houses controlling river trade.
Character
Farmlands, river valleys, ancient settlements
Agricultural WealthRiver TradeOld Nobility
Bound God
Sarrum — domain of Earth
Peoples
Three dominant ancestries share Brauogi: Goblin, Hobgoblin, and Minotaur (Sarrum himself is depicted as a Minotaur — Brauogi's Minotaurs are his cultural cousins). The three-way of Earth-domain politics is more cooperative than feud-prone; they trade rather than fight.
Magitrain
On the southern continental network. Several lines through the river valleys; the line to Sumendar carries the bulk of grain-for-iron trade.
