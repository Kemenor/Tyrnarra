Lautara
Commerce Domain

Most of the continent's overland trade passes through Lautara at some point in its journey.

At a Glance

Etymology
Basque lautada (plain, flatland) → lauta → drifted to Lautara. The silk-road plains where the caravans gather and the rivers run.
Position
South-central Talan. Neighbours: Midarra (north, with the Order of Steam bay opening northward between two enclosing headlands; the bay is a freshwater sub-body of the freshwater Midarra; Rika Tikur the island-city sits inside it), Sumendar (west, with the Order of Steam controlling the bay's west coast and its uninhabited north-west mountain-headland, and Tahu Tangata fronting the south-west of the bay), Lioaru (south/southwest), Ezkudon (south/southeast), Egulon (east), Zuzental (northeast). The Dreaming Cape itself dominates the bay's other coast (south, east, and the north-east headland where the Cape's capital sits).
Terrain
Rolling cultivated land cut by a dense river network; the rivers all converge on the great inland lake at the foot of Helgafjall, the sacred snow-capped peak that rises from the lake's centre. Mountains rim the western edge facing the Order of Steam bay. Eastern-cultivated highlands at the western margin (housing Emarrea). Northern Midarra coast for sea-trade; the long inland caravan-and-train network for everything else. Desert is not a Lautara register; Lioaru carries that to the south.
Character
Silk-road caravans, lake-port assembly, river-junction warehousing
Silk RoadEastern RegisterTrade Spine
Cultural register
Eastern, domain-wide. Silk-road merchant culture: caravan staging-yards, lake-port assembly, river-junction warehousing, train-yard depots. Merkavar reads in the Samarkand/Bukhara register. Sub-regions vary the intensity (Emarrea full kotokoe-and-kitsune; Itsasalda and Atarialda silk-road merchant-Eastern; Azkataria agricultural-Eastern; Dreaming Cape modern-Eastern mercantile-port) but the Eastern register holds across the domain.
Peoples
Three dominant ancestries anchor here: Kitsune (centred in Emarrea), Vishkanya, and Halfling. The mercantile mix runs cosmopolitan; a Merkavar warehouse-floor at peak hour holds half the continent in one room.
Bound God
Jianna, domain of Commerce
Magitrain
The central hub of the southern continental network. Merkavar is where the southern rail lines converge; rolling stock manufactured by the Order of Steam reaches the network via the Rika Tikur ports, and passenger lines fan out from Merkavar east across Atarialda to Egulon, Ezkudon, and Zuzental.
God's City
Merkavar
The Market City

Merkavar is its own city-state-with-territory: the city, the great inland lake that surrounds it, and Helgafjall (Icelandic helgr "holy" + fjall "mountain"), the sacred snow-capped peak that rises from the lake's centre. The lake-city is a destination, not a port; ships dock at the Midarra coast, and goods cross to Merkavar by river-craft or caravan-train. Pilgrim trails wind up Helgafjall's slopes; the snow on its peak never fully melts, and the lake at its foot has never frozen in record.

Itsasalda →
Basque itsasoaldea "the seaside region" → vowel-loss to Itsasalda
Substantial central-northern sub-region of Lautara, Halfling-dominated through every working layer (rail, river, coast, dock). Governed by the Conductor-Magistracy: five seated Magistrates (Senior, Foreign, Coast, River, Rail) ride portfolio circuits with no fixed seat, fed by a formal Halfling Railworkers' Association pipeline. De-facto capital Bellhalt. Holds Itsasalda's half of Hringseyja, the circular island bisected with the Legea Empire along the Quietline. See dedicated page.

Rika Tikur
Icelandic ríkur (rich) + tígulegur (splendid), "Rich and Splendid"; merchant-port self-naming
Independent island-city inside the Order of Steam bay, a freshwater sub-body of the freshwater Midarra, opening only northward to the open Midarra between two enclosing headlands: the Order of Steam's north-west mountain-headland (uninhabited) and the Dreaming Cape's north-east headland (where the Cape's capital sits). Going clockwise from the bay-mouth, the bay's coast belongs to Order of Steam (west), Tahu Tangata (south-west), and the Dreaming Cape (south, east, NE headland; the Cape holds the bigger share). Rika Tikur handles the bulk industrial freight from the OoS volcanic-belt manufactories; goods process here and ship out through the bay-mouth into the open Midarra, or travel onward to Merkavar by river.

The Dreaming Cape →
Modern English; named by Adventurer-Era travellers for the local dreaming sub-state called the drift
Substantial coastal sub-region forming the NE headland of the freshwater Order of Steam bay and wrapping its south and east coasts. Capital Moonwatch on the NE headland; high temple the Twin Lantern doubles as the bay-mouth lighthouse. Governed by the Cape Theocracy of Bikiargi: a gynocracy headed by paired identical-twin Bright Voice + Dark Voice, with the Midnight Sisters as executive layer; selection ritual is the Lunarcall. Signature exports: the five-tier dream-trade and the Wakehelm paired ship-and-dream-pilot service (Midarra experts). Bordered by Tahu Tangata (W, across the Markaa river) and Itsasalda (E + S, across a mountain range and a smaller river); Itsasalda extends between the Cape and Emarrea. See dedicated page.

Azkataria →
Basque azoka (market) + -tar(ra) (people-of), "market-folk"
The agricultural feeder belt south of Merkavar, wrapping the southern face of the city-state's territory and holding the southern approach to Helgafjall. Governed by the Cellarsworn, the chartered League of Cellar Houses (Council meets at Roskelstein at the trailhead). Three Pilgrim Houses (Fornbar, Lestastad, Starnasal) hold trail-charters and produce the Pilgrim Reserves under the Threshold Seal; three Cellar Houses (Goldenleaf, Sundwell, Highvine) carry the bulk Cellar Mark volume. Atarialda
Basque atari (threshold, gateway) + aldea (region), "the threshold-region"
The eastern arm of Lautara, holding the overland trade routes toward Egulon, Ezkudon, and Zuzental. The gateway-region: caravans leaving Merkavar east cross Atarialda, and Crossroads sits at its northeast corner as the literal atari of the domain.

Emarrea →
Basque ema (soft, gentle) + arrea (tawny) → "the soft tawny land"
The kitsune kingdom on Lautara's western margin, neighbouring Hareaveldi (Lioaru), Tahu Tangata (Sumendar), and Wildreach (Ezkudon). Capital Biozuri, mountain resort Kawaakari, central district Heartplaza. Notable terrain: Yukihime-no-mori, the Snow Princess Forest of pink-blossomed plum orchards. Governed by the Heartcourt, a polycule of 4–9 elected Hearts. See dedicated page.
