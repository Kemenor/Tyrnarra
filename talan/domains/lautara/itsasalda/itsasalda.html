At a Glance

Etymology
Basque itsasoaldea "the seaside region" (itsaso "sea" + aldea "region") → vowel-loss to Itsasalda, "seaside." Deep-old, predates the modern polity by centuries.
Position
Substantial central-northern sub-region of Lautara. Open Midarra to the N (access partially blocked by the Muino-saila hilly range; port-towns sit in the gaps); the Dreaming Cape to the NW (across the Vordfjall mountain range and the Smáa river); Atarialda to the E; Azkataria to the SE; Merkavar's lake-and-Helgafjall territory to the S; Emarrea to the SW across the Markaa river. Offshore: Hringseyja, the circular island bisected with the Legea Empire.
Terrain
Open coastal-and-plains country crossed by multiple rivers running south to Merkavar's lake; rolling rail-spur country between Bellhalt and Atarialda; the Muino-saila hill-range along the north coast; Vordfjall mountains along the Cape border in the NW.

De-Facto Capital
Bellhalt
the rail-halt at the largest Muino-saila gap · HRA regional seat

Bellhalt sits where the rail-line meets the principal river and the Midarra coast at once. The Halfling-English compound carries both the rail-halt and the signal-bell tradition: the bell rings, the conductors halt, the cargo is sorted, the next train carries it on. The HRA's Itsasalda regional headquarters is here; the Senior Magistrate's correspondence address is here; the largest cross-portfolio Magistrate-court when more than one Magistrate is in town is here.

The Conductor-Magistracy

Itsasalda is governed by the Conductor-Magistracy, a distributed institutional polity with no fixed seat. Five seated Magistrates ride portfolio circuits across Itsasalda's coast, rivers, rail, customs, and foreign-affairs work, holding court while they travel and corresponding between beats. A ship that needs a writ, a port that needs a ruling, a river-village with a dispute waits for the relevant Magistrate to ride through; the working law is whatever she finds on arrival and whatever she decides before riding on.

The Magistracy formalised in the mid-Adventurer Era, when the Legea Empire's missionary programme pushed onto Hringseyja and the region needed a polity with diplomatic standing to negotiate the partition. Before then, the Halfling Railworkers' Association already ran Itsasalda's working infrastructure; the Magistracy gave that infrastructural reach a governance face that the Empire could send envoys to.

The Magistrate Title

Seated members of the Conductor-Magistracy are titled Magistrate in working speech; official documents use the portfolio prefix as the full title (e.g., Foreign Magistrate, Senior Magistrate, River Magistrate). The hyphenated Conductor- attaches to the polity, not to individuals. A Magistrate's writ travels in her satchel; her court convenes wherever she dismounts; her correspondence finds her at the next bell.

The Five Portfolios

Senior Magistrate
First among equals · peer-elected

Elected from the seated body by the other four Magistrates. Holds no separate beat; rides as needed to mediate between portfolios, witness the Lunarcall-equivalent moments of the Magistracy's own working year, and host the cross-portfolio court at Bellhalt. The Senior's correspondence address is the closest thing the polity has to a fixed seat.

Foreign Magistrate
Hringseyja partition + sea-travel commerce

The Quietline first, the wider Itsasalda foreign-affairs portfolio second. The only Magistrate authorised to leave the continent by sea on duty; the only Magistrate whose beat crosses the Itsasalda land border at all. Her embassy-presence sits at Itsasalda's nearest port to Hringseyja; her circuit rides the half-island.

Coast Magistrate
Muino-saila port-gaps + coast-customs

The port-towns along the hilly north coast and the coast-customs work. Anti-smuggling patrols, dock-fee arbitration, the routine ship-writ ports need to keep moving. Her circuit follows the working coast; her bell rings at each port-gap in turn.

River Magistrate
Inland river-routes to Merkavar

The river-network from Midarra coast to Merkavar's lake: barge-captain disputes, river-village arbitration, the river-and-coast handoff at each port. Most river-villages see her once or twice a year. Her court convenes on a barge if the disputants are barge-folk.

Rail Magistrate
Itsasalda rail-spur + HRA labour relations

The Itsasalda spur of the southern rail network, the HRA-labour-side relations, the rail-junction settlements between Bellhalt and Atarialda. Where the Magistracy and the union meet most directly; the Rail Magistrate's portfolio carries the diplomatic weight of speaking for both layers at once when needed.

The HRA Pipeline

The Conductor-Magistracy is fed by the Halfling Railworkers' Association along a fixed promotion path. Magistrates are recruited from senior HRA members who have come up through the union's working ranks. The pipeline is formal; the candidacy is continental.

The Conductor-Magistracy is structurally the HRA's promotion ceiling. The non-HRA pipeline (river-captains, port-keepers) is closed by current standing rule.

Any senior and trusted HRA member from across the continent may stand for Magistrate candidacy: the pipeline is continental, not regional. In practice most seated Magistrates come up through the Itsasaldan branch, where the HRA's regional structure puts candidates closer to the work and more well known to the seated body; but HRA seniors from other branches do sit on the Magistracy from time to time, and the cross-branch presence is one of the things that keeps the HRA continental rather than provincial.

The Association is consequently a two-layer institution: labour (the working union, continental in reach) and governance (the seated Magistracy in Itsasalda). This two-layer character is unique on Talan. The HRA is the most powerful Halfling institution on the continent, and the Cellarsworn and other Lautara polities have begun watching the Association in ways they never watched a pure labour body.

Hringseyja and the Quietline

Offshore in Midarra, the circular island of Hringseyja (Icelandic hringr "ring" + eyja "island") is bisected between Itsasalda and the Legea Empire by a fixed partition: the Quietline. Each side governs its half. The line is not contested but it is not friendly. Itsasalda's half-island sits under a pattern of uneasy coexistence: small settled populations on both sides, occasional border-incidents at the line, formal embassies across by announced protocol only. Locals on the partition-side villages call the line the Edge, and the working life of those villages runs in close sight of the foreign jurisdiction across the road.

The Quietline was sworn in a mid-Adventurer-Era year (canon-pending) between the Senior Magistrate of the day and the Legea Empire's regional governor. Both sides have kept it for generations since. The Foreign Magistrate's standing job is to keep the incidents small.

Itsasalda's half
The Halfling Side

Halfling-dominated villages, the Foreign Magistrate's embassy-court at the half-island's principal port, fishing and modest river-mouth trade. The partition-side market operates within yards of the Edge and is constantly under the Magistrate's eye. The Halfling residents have lived alongside their Empire neighbours for so long that some extended households were cut in half by the original partition; small-scale family-visits across the Quietline by announced permission are routine.

The Empire's half
The Faith Side

A small permanent Legea Empire human population on the other side of the Quietline, with their own port-village and a modest mission of the Divine Faith maintained for the local community. The Empire's regional governor's office handles the Empire-side. Cross-line contact is constant in small ways: smuggling, partition-cut marriage-line disputes, the occasional missionary venturing across to Itsasalda proper (always politely redirected by the Foreign Magistrate).

The River-Trade

Multiple smaller rivers from the Itsasalda interior carry the most-direct inland-water route between the Midarra coast and Merkavar's lake. The Itsasalda river-trade is the working spine of the southern network's water side: barge-captains run the routes; rail at Bellhalt and the other port-junctions handles the cross-modal transfer; Vishkanya customs-clerks at each port-mouth manage the river-customs work that pays the Magistracy's running cost.

The Markaa river (Itsasalda's southern border with Emarrea) is the largest of the system and the route by which Emarrea's kotokoe trade-goods reach Merkavar through Itsasaldan waters. The River Magistrate's circuit follows the rivers themselves; her court convenes on a moored barge when the disputants are barge-folk.

Peoples and Faith

Halflings are the dominant population at every working layer: the rail-crews who run the southern network's Itsasalda spur, the river-barge captains who work the Merkavar routes, the dock-workers in the Muino-saila gap-ports, the Conductors who train toward the Magistracy, the seated Magistrates themselves. The HRA's Itsasalda regional offices employ thousands; the smaller HRA chapter-houses ring through every rail-junction settlement in the region.

Vishkanya anchor the customs-clerk and court-scrivener class attached to each Magistrate's working retinue. A Magistrate's court is the Magistrate plus her clerk and her scrivener; the Vishkanya tradition of numerical precision and ledger-keeping suits both roles, and the customs-clerk pipeline runs heavily through Vishkanya house-lineages in much the same way that Azkataria's Cellar-Master pipeline does. Cosmopolitan mix in the port-towns, dock-work, and rail-junction settlements.

Faith stack: Jianna primary, the working faith of the ports and the river-trade; Shuun of Floteyn acknowledged among river-barge captains and coast-pilots in particular (the water-domain's reach into the Midarra river-system is part of Itsasaldan working-faith); Bikiargi shrines mark the lunar festivals as they do in any Lautaran town, with no special Cape-style concentration.

Live Tensions

Three arguments run quietly across Itsasalda.

The Quietline Incidents

Small flare-ups at the partition continue on a multi-year rhythm; the Foreign Magistrate's standing job is to keep them small. Most are smuggling, partition-cut marriage-line disputes, or the occasional Legea Empire missionary venturing off-island into Itsasalda proper. Each generation has its own canonical incident that the village-grandmothers can recite the resolution of.

The HRA's Political Weight

With the Magistracy as the Association's promotion ceiling, the HRA has become a continental power-bloc that other Lautara polities eye uneasily. The Cellarsworn in particular have argued for freight-rate caps that the HRA's autumn vote at Merkavar keeps blocking. The argument runs in every autumn assembly; the rate-caps have not passed since the Magistracy formalised.

The Non-HRA Pipeline

River-captains and port-keepers have argued for generations that the formal HRA-only pipeline excludes their crafts from the Magistracy. The seated Magistrates have kept the pipeline closed by standing rule, though several Magistrates came up through rail-work that included river-postings, and the closure is felt more strongly by the river-captains than the rail-workers. River-captain associations periodically petition for a separate pipeline; the petitions are received politely and shelved.

Once a year, on a date the Quietline-Founding-Day commemorates (canon-pending), the Foreign Magistrate of Itsasalda and the Legea Empire's regional governor on Hringseyja meet for tea. They meet at the partition-line itself, at a small stone table that has been there since the Quietline was sworn, with one chair on each side. They drink from the same pot of tea that one of them has brewed and the other has approved. They speak for an hour. Neither side records what is said. Both sides know what is said. They part. The table is washed by the partition-side village's eldest Halfling grandmother and the Empire-side village's eldest Faith-elder, on alternate years.

The locals say the tea is what keeps the Quietline quiet. The Magistrates and the governors say nothing public about it. Some years one or the other dies between teas, and the next year's tea is shared with whatever new Magistrate or governor has been assigned; the table is the constant. They say the table has not failed a tea in forty years.

What the tea actually does is the standing question. The Foreign Magistrate's clerk insists the tea is purely ceremonial, an annual confirmation that the line still holds. The partition-side villagers insist the tea is the working diplomacy and the embassies are the ceremony. The Empire-side villagers do not say. The tea continues.
