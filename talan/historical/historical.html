The Fallen Civilisations of Talan
Three civilisations defined the world before the world remembered itself. Each shaped the continent in their century, then ended — by force, by overreach, or by transformation into something unrecognisable. What survives of them is the world we live in.
✦

Why This Section Exists

Modern Talan is not a clean slate. The kingdoms, the rail network, the dwarven script that still mortars old place-names — most of what feels timeless about the continent was built, broken, or birthed by civilisations that no longer exist as themselves. The history page tells the chronological story; this section tells the institutional story — who these civilisations were as polities, what they actually did, and what they left.

Three civilisations have earned dedicated pages so far. Others will follow as the canon makes room.

The Fallen

The Golden Empire
560 MR – 1325 MR · Dwarven · ~70% of Talan
The largest mortal empire in recorded history. Seven centuries of consistent rule, common law, roads, and the dwarven scholarly script that still fossilises Talanese place-names. Collapsed in four years when the breach came. The Emperor's reign is the cultural foundation half the modern kingdoms trace themselves to.

The Storveldi Denbora
Mid-Gods' Era · mortal civilisation · fall = the Blackened Lands
A mortal people who settled atop Elden ruins, claimed Elden descent for prestige, and ran a generations-long programme of divine self-engineering. Their ruling class self-elevated en masse; two of their sovereigns reached Minor God status by integration. They killed Tani. Their homeland was annihilated in the Week of Crimson Rain; the cursed scar is the modern Lost Kingdom. The Azarketi descend from them.

The Elden
6000 GR – 2945 GR · neither mortal nor fully divine · vanished
A high-tech civilisation that dominated Talan for over three thousand years. Built the Androids no one has since matched. Vanished on a single day in 2945 GR, cities intact, libraries full, hearths still warm. Their ruins are the source of modern Magitech. Whatever they became (see The Binding for the truth) is not a topic of polite scholarship.

What They Have In Common

Each of the three reached a height no other civilisation of its time approached, and each ended in a way the gods did not predict. Two of them — Storveldi and the Elden — were the catalyst for the two largest cosmic events in Talan's history (the Week of Crimson Rain and the Dark Era respectively). The third, the Golden Empire, looked like the calm between catastrophes. Whether it actually was is a question more interesting than most scholars realise.

Their fingerprints are everywhere in modern Talan. Dwarven script in your kingdom's law-books. Elden rail principles under your Magitrain. Storveldi blood in the Azarketi who run the harbour. The continent has no clean foundation — it has layers, and these three are most of what's beneath the soil.
